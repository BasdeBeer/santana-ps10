# Clutch System (Section 7C) - Detailed Service Manual

## General Description (Page 7C-2)

### Clutch Type and Operation

The Santana PS10 is equipped with a **diaphragm-spring clutch of dry single type**. The diaphragm-spring is of a tapered-finger type, which is a solid ring in the outer diameter part, with a series of tapered fingers pointing inward.

The disc, carrying four torsional coil springs, is positioned on the transmission input shaft with an involute spline fit.

### Clutch Engagement Mechanics

The clutch cover is secured to the flywheel, and carries the diaphragm-spring in such a way that the peripheral edge part of the spring pushes on the flywheel, pressure plate and the disc in between. When the clutch release bearing is held back, this is the engaged condition of the clutch.

**Disengagement Process:**

Depressing the clutch pedal causes the release bearing to advance and pushes on the tips of the tapered fingers of the diaphragm-spring. This pulls the pressure plate away from the flywheel. Thereby interrupting the flow of drive from flywheel through clutch disc to transmission input shaft.

## Diagnosis and Troubleshooting (Page 7C-3)

### Condition: Slipping

**Possible Causes:**
- Oil disc facing
- Worn disc facing
- Weakened disc spring
- Warped pressure plate or flywheel
- Improper clutch pedal free travel

**Correction:** Adjust and replace clutch, facings if needed. Replace components as indicated.

### Condition: Dragging Clutch

**Possible Causes:**
- Improper clutch pedal free travel
- Weakened diaphragm spring or worn spring tip
- Rusted primary shaft splines
- Damaged or worn splines at transmission primary shaft
- Excessively wobbly clutch disc
- Disc facings broken or dirty with oil

**Correction:** Adjust free travel. Replace components as needed.

### Condition: Clutch Vibration

**Possible Causes:**
- Glazed (glass-like) disc facings
- Disc facings dirty with oil
- Wobbly clutch disc or poor facing contact
- Weakened torsion springs in clutch disc
- Cracked disc rivets loose
- Distorted flywheel or pressure plate surfaces
- Weakened or loosened engine silentblock, nut or bolt

**Correction:** Replace disc, covers, or flywheel. Retighten or replace engine mountings.

### Condition: Noisy Clutch

**Possible Causes:**
- Broken or worn release bearing
- Primary shaft front bearing worn down
- Excessive rattle of clutch disc hub
- Cracked clutch disc
- Pressure plate and diaphragm-spring rattling

**Correction:** Replace affected components.

### Condition: Grabbing Clutch

**Possible Causes:**
- Disc facings soaked with oil
- Disc facings excessively worn
- Rivet heads showing out at facing
- Weakened torsion springs

**Correction:** Replace disc facings or complete assembly.

## On-Vehicle Service Procedures (Page 7C-4 to 7C-9)

### Clutch Pedal Height and Free Travel (Page 7C-4)

**Clutch Pedal Height Adjustment:**

1. Adjust height (A) using screw (1)

**Specification - Pedal Height:** 140 mm

**Clutch Pedal Free Travel Adjustment:**

2. Adjust free travel using nuts (2)

**Play Specifications:**
- **(B) Play between piston and rod:** 1.5 mm
- **(C) Free pedal play:** 15 ± 20 mm

**Adjustment Procedure:**

3. Once adjustments have been made, note the observations and torque
4. **Torque for rod setscrews:** 2 ± 2.5 Kg-m (20 ± 50 Nm)

**Note:** To clean and dry the parts use fluff free cloths.

### Draining the Clutch Circuit (Page 7C-5)

**Important Note:**
During the operation, fill the tank with liquid so as to avoid air entering the system. Only use recommended hydraulic liquid.

**Procedure:**

1. Attach a piece of flexible and transparent piping (1) to slave cylinder bleeder screw (2)
2. Place the free end of the piping in a glass container (3) in order to collect the clutch fluid. Make sure the end is always submerged during the bleeding
3. Loosen the bleeder screw (2)
4. Push the clutch pedal slowly, pausing briefly when the pedal is up
5. Stop pumping when the liquid comes out without air
6. Keep the piping end submerged and tighten bleeder screw (2) at the same time as pushing the pedal down
7. Adjust clutch fluid level to the tank maximum (Max)
8. Check clutch pedal height and travel (see 7C-4) and adjust if necessary

### Main Clutch Cylinder Replacement (Page 7C-6)

**Removal:**

1. Release liquid inlet and outlet pipes
2. Remove pedal support setscrews from dashboard
3. Remove support (1) with pedal and cylinder
4. Clean assembly on a workbench and removing the cylinder fixing elements from the support and pedal

**Installation:**

1. To install the main clutch cylinder, reverse the order of dismantling, noting the indicated torque and the clutch pedal height adjustment (see 7C-4)

**Torque Specifications:**
- **Torque for main cylinder control rod fixing nuts:** 2 ± 2.5 Kg-m (20 ± 25 Nm)

### Auxiliary Clutch Cylinder Replacement (Page 7C-7)

**Removal:**

Similar to main cylinder, following the manufacturer's prescribed sequence.

**Installation:**

Reverse removal procedures, noting specified torque values and fluid level adjustment.

## Required Service Materials (Page 7C-15)

**Approved Hydraulic Fluid:**
- Only use recommended hydraulic liquid (DOT 4 specification typically)

**Sealants and Gaskets:**
- Approved sealants for cylinder connections
- Replacement gaskets for all dismantled components

## System Capacity

The clutch circuit typically holds approximately 250-300 ml of hydraulic fluid. Ensure proper refill levels during maintenance procedures.

## Maintenance Schedule

**Regular Checks:**
- Verify pedal play monthly
- Check for fluid leaks quarterly
- Inspect disc condition annually or at 60,000 km intervals
- Complete system fluid flush every 3 years or as specified

**Critical Inspection Points:**
- Hydraulic line integrity
- Cylinder seal condition
- Disc facing wear
- Pressure plate wear
- Spring fatigue
