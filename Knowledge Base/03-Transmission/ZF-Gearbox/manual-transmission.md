# ZF Manual Transmission (LT-85) - Detailed Specifications

## Overview

The ZF LT-85 is a fully synchronized five-speed manual transmission with helicidal forward gears and straight-cut reverse gear. This manual covers repair, assembly, and service procedures for the Santana PS10.

## Transmission Architecture

### Shaft Configuration

**Primary Input Shaft**
- Connected directly to engine clutch
- Carries input gears for all forward speeds
- Helicidal gear construction

**Main Output Shaft**
- Carries output gears for forward speeds
- Reverse gear selector mechanism
- Direct connection to transfer case

**Counter Shaft (Auxiliary Shaft)**
- Provides torque path for 3rd, 4th, and 5th gears
- Parallel to primary shaft

**Reverse Shaft**
- Straight-cut design for reverse engagement
- Independent selector mechanism

### Gear Specifications

#### Forward Gear Ratios

| Gear | Drive Gear | Driven Gear | Ratio | Speed Reduction |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|-----------------|
| 1st | 37 | 14 | 36/13 or 37/14 | 2.768 |
| 2nd | 26 | 25 | 30/19 or 26/25 | 2.643 |
| 3rd | - | - | 1.519 | 1.519 |
| 4th | - | - | 1.040 | 1.040 |
| 5th | 33 | 19 | 19/33 | 0.576 |

**Reverse Gear:** Straight-cut design, non-synchronized

### Synchroniser System

**Type:** POCKET-type electro-eroded synchronizers
**Configuration:** Grooved couplings
**Function:** Prevents slipping during gear engagement under harsh conditions
**Coverage:** All forward speeds (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th)
**Reverse:** Non-synchronized straight engagement

### Clutch Interface

**Input Connection:** Involute spline fit on transmission input shaft
**Pressure Plate Interface:** Four torsional coil springs
**Engagement Type:** Diaphragm-spring clutch (see Section 7C)

## Service and Repair

### On-Vehicle Service Procedures

#### Gear Lever and Transfer Lever (Page 7A-6)

**Dismantling Procedure:**

1. Remove screws from front carpet to access tunnel cover (1)
2. Remove lower boot clamp (3)
3. Move boot (2) and tunnel cover upwards
4. Press case cover (4) and turn it left to extract gear lever assembly and boot
5. For transfer shift lever: Remove lever knob (5), remove front carpet, raise boots and flanges upwards
6. Remove three cover screws from lever case (9)
7. Extract transfer shift lever assembly and boot

**Inspection Points:**

- Check for erosion on friction zones, lever supports, and cases
- Check boots for breakages and replace if necessary
- Check state of screws and spring pins (10) - replace if necessary
- If screws are removed, apply recommended sealant to threads
- Tighten to specified torque

**Torque Specifications for Gear Lever:**

- **Position lever side screws torque:** 1.7 Kg-m (17 Nm)

#### Assembly

1. Apply recommended grease to lever seating zone
2. Install new boot fitting and tighten firmly
3. Fit remaining components in reverse order
4. Apply indicated torque to all fasteners

**Torque for plate screws:** 0.6 Kg-m (6 Nm)

### Transmission Removal and Installation (Page 7A-8 to 7A-10)

#### Dismantling

**Preparation:**
- Remove gear lever and transfer gear lever assembly (see above)
- Disconnect negative battery cable

**Caution:** In vehicles with common rail engine, do not disconnect battery for up to 10 seconds after ignition is turned off.

**Under Hood Procedures:**

1. If transmission is being removed:
   - Drain transmission oil by removing plug (2)
   - Clean oil from plug thread and housings, apply recommended sealant
   - Assemble plugs and tighten to specified torque

**Torque for plug (1):** 4.5 Kg-m (45 Nm)
**Torque for plug (2):** 2.3 Kg-m (23 Nm)

**On Lift:**

2. Disconnect forward section of exhaust pipe from turbo-compressor gas outlet elbow joint

3. Disconnect speedometer cable (7) and ground cable

4. Disconnect auxiliary cylinder clutch pipe and cover ports to prevent liquid loss

5. Disconnect parking brake conditioning rod by removing spring pin

**Removal Process:**

6. If using hydraulic table, place it on lower part of 5th gear case (reinforced zone) if possible, screwing mechanism plate to housing threaded pivots

   **Warning:** It is recommended to use a coupling system on the hydraulic table for greater security during extraction of transmission and transfer box assembly.

7. Tighten lifting mechanism without forcing
8. Remove transmission holding plate
9. Detach plates with corresponding silentblocks
10. Place a holding system for engine
11. Disconnect reverse speed and double traction couplers, disconnect electric branch of rolled flange and small breather hoses placed on rear of engine
12. Remove screws which join engine and transmission and transfer box assembly
13. Detach transmission and transfer box assembly until end of primary shaft is completely free of pressure plate diaphragm
    **Caution:** This operation should be carried out making sure that no component is damaged or forced, disconnect any that is seen to be damaged.

#### Assembly

1. Apply recommended grease film to main shaft end and grooving
2. **Torque for fixing nuts - clutch case to flywheel case:** 4.5 ± 5.5 Kg-m (45 ± 55 Nm)
3. **Torque for fixing screws - clutch case to flywheel case:** 4 ± 6 Kg-m (40 ± 60 Nm)

**Note:** Check that transmission silentblock holding plates are in good condition; if not, replace them with new ones.

- **Torque for gear box holding plates:** 7 ± 10 Kg-m (70 ± 100 Nm)
- **Torque for silentblock fixing screws:** 7 ± 10 Kg-m (70 ± 100 Nm)

3. Fix wiring with rolled clamps and connect the electric couplers
4. **Torque for propeller shafts flange fixing nuts:** 2.8 ± 3.5 Kg-m (28 ± 35 Nm)
5. **Torque for propeller shafts constant velocity joint fixing screws:** 3 ± 4 Kg-m (30 ± 40 Nm)

6. Purge the clutch liquid (see Section 7C-5)
7. If oil has been emptied from transmission and transfer box, refill to correct level (see 0B-15 v 0B-16)
8. Check everything is working correctly and there is no leakage
