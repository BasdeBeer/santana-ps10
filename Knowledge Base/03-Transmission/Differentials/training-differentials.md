## Differential System Overview

The Santana PS10 features both front and rear differential systems for all-wheel drive capability. This document provides training information on differential maintenance and service.

## Service Procedures Overview

### Oil Change Procedure

1. **Raise Vehicle** - Secure on jack stands
2. **Locate Drain Plug** - Typically on bottom of differential housing
3. **Drain Old Oil** - Allow complete drainage
4. **Replace Drain Plug** - Clean and reinstall with new washer
5. **Refill with Specified Oil** - Use OEM-approved differential oil
6. **Check Level** - Verify on sight glass (if equipped) or by filler plug
7. **Road Test** - Ensure smooth operation

### Inspection Points

During service, inspect for:
- Leaks at housing seams
- Worn or damaged seals
- Proper fluid level
- Fluid condition (color, smell, contamination)
- Ring and pinion condition
- Bearing operation (noise/vibration)

## Common Differential Issues

| Problem | Possible Cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Oil Leaks | Worn seals, loose bolts, cracks | Replace seals, tighten fasteners |
| Noise | Low fluid, bearing wear, gear damage | Check fluid level, inspect gears |
| Poor Traction | Limited-slip failure, worn clutches | Replace limited-slip unit if equipped |
| Vibration | Imbalance, worn bearings, damaged gears | Inspect and replace as needed |
| Grinding Sound | Gear damage, metal in oil | Complete fluid change, inspect gears |

## Front Differential Service

### Location and Access

- Located at front axle assembly
- Accessible from below vehicle
- Requires axles to be partially removed for seal replacement

### Service Highlights

- Oil changes every 30,000 km
- Seal inspection at each service
- Bearing condition monitoring
- Axle shaft seal replacement if leaking

---

## Rear Differential Service

### Location and Access

- Located in rear axle housing
- Accessible from below vehicle
- Direct drain and fill access

### Service Highlights

- Oil change frequency: 30,000 km
- Monitoring for leaks at axle seals
- Limited-slip clutch inspection (if equipped)
- Ring and pinion condition assessment

## Rotating Pivots Lubrication

The maintenance schedule includes "rotating pivots" service. This refers to:

1. **Universal Joints** - Propeller shaft connections
2. **CV Joints** - Constant velocity joints (front drive)
3. **Differential Bearings** - Integral to differential operation

All of these require:
- Regular inspection for leaks
- Proper lubrication
- Boot condition checks (CV joints)
- Grease replenishment if equipped with grease fittings

## Limited-Slip Differential (if equipped)

### Function

- Automatically limits speed differential between wheels
- Improves traction on slippery surfaces
- Maintains standard differential function on firm surfaces

### Maintenance Considerations

- Requires special limited-slip differential oil
- Friction modifier additives necessary
- Complete fluid change per schedule
- Do not use standard differential oil

### Service Procedure Differences

- Use exact OEM-specified fluid
- May require special mixing procedures
- Follow manufacturer's break-in procedures
- Monitor for chatter (normal but audible)

## All-Wheel Drive System Integration

The differentials are part of the complete all-wheel drive system:

**System Components:**
- Manual transmission primary drive
- Transfer case power distribution
- Front differential assembly
- Rear differential assembly
- Propeller shafts connecting components
- Axle shafts to wheels

**Maintenance Philosophy:**
- All components must be serviced on schedule
- Proper fluid types critical for system longevity
- Differential function affects overall vehicle handling

## Safety and Best Practices

### During Service

1. Secure vehicle with proper jacks and stands
2. Use drip pan to catch spilled oil
3. Allow hot fluid to cool before handling
4. Never work under unsupported vehicle
5. Proper personal protective equipment (PPE)

### Fluid Disposal

- Used differential oil is hazardous waste
- Dispose per environmental regulations
- Never pour into drains or soil
- Use certified recycling facilities

### Torque Procedures

- Always use calibrated torque wrench
- Apply torque in recommended sequence (if multi-bolt)
- Recheck critical fasteners after test drive
- Use new gaskets/seals (do not reuse)
