# Owner Maintenance

## Routine Maintenance Tasks

5. MAINTENANCE BY THE OWNER
WINDSCREEN WIPERS

WARNING
• Do not touch bulbs immediately
after turning them off, because they
could cause burns. Replace them
after they go cold.
• Be careful when manipulating
halogen
headlights.
The
pressurised
halogen gas it
contains could make them burst if
hit or dropped.
• Hold the new bulbs with a clean
cloth. Skin oil could cause
overheating and bulb bursting
when turning it on.

To change a windscreen wiper, lift the arm
from the screen, press the catch ( signalled
with an arrow in the illustration), push the
wiper in the opposite direction of the arm
and unhook it. Place the new set of
scrapers on the arm, hook the swivelling
holder and push to hook until the scraper
clasps with the clip. Assemble scrapers
that always fulfil original specifications
exactly.

CAUTION
The need to frequently
change a bulb, means there could be
a failure in the electric system.

## Basic Checks

Regular owner maintenance includes:
- Checking engine oil level
- Verifying coolant level
- Inspecting brake fluid level
- Checking tire pressures and condition
- Inspecting windscreen wipers and belts

Perform these checks monthly or before long trips.
