## You Need

- **4 pins** minimum (one per cruise control function)
- **Wire:** 0.75mm² automotive wire is ideal (sits in the middle of the 0.5-1.0mm² range)
- **No special crimping tool required** if you're soldering. The open barrel design accepts solder well.

## Tips for Installation

1. The pin has a wire barrel and an insulation barrel. When soldering, tin the wire, place it in the wire barrel, and heat from below. Let solder wick into the barrel.
2. After soldering, fold the insulation barrel tabs over the wire insulation for strain relief, then heat shrink over the whole joint.
3. The pin clicks into the connector housing and is retained by a small latch. Insert straight, push until it clicks.
4. To remove a pin if you make a mistake, you'll need a pin extraction tool or a very thin flat-blade screwdriver to release the latch.
