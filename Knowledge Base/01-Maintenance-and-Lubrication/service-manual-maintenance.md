# Service Manual Maintenance and Lubrication (Section 0B)

**Source:** Santana PS10 Service Manual (791044-C1I), Pages 6-33

## Overview

This section covers all scheduled maintenance, lubrication requirements, and service procedures for the Santana PS10. Follow the maintenance schedule based on either odometer reading or time (whichever comes first).

## Severe Driving Conditions

If the vehicle is driven under severe conditions, follow additional maintenance intervals as defined below.

### Severe Driving Codes

- **A** = Short and frequent trips
- **B** = Driving through muddy paths and/or grounds
- **C** = Driving through paths and/or dusty grounds
- **D** = Driving on very cold weather and/or salty paths
- **E** = Short and frequent trips under cold weather
- **G** = Towing driving/ towing/ high speed driving/ Temperatures above 40°C / Lubricants and fuel of low quality
- **H** = Towing
- **I** = Hired Vehicles

## Engine Service Details

### Accessory Belt Inspection and Adjustment (Item 1.1)

**Belt Tension Specifications:**
- Generator and water pump belt: **140 ± 5 Hz** (measured with frequency meter ref. 00000A01484)
- A/A compressor belt: **160 ± 10 Hz** (measured with frequency meter ref. 00000A01484)

For replacement procedures, see Section 6A-26 and 6A-27.

### Timing Belt (Item 1.2)

For timing belt substitution procedures, see Section 6A-28.

### Engine Oil and Filter (Item 1.3)

**Recommended Oil Specifications:**
- **Viscosity:** SAE 10W/40
- **Standard:** API SJ/CF

**Note:** If the recommended oil is not suitable to the climate of the country where the vehicle is being utilized, see viscosities table and use the required SANTANA MOTOR oil.

**Engine Oil Amounts:**
- Total Amount (Drained motor): 7.3 litres
- Including filter: 7.0 litres
- Without filters: 5.9 litres

**Oil Change Procedure:**
1. Use adequate container to collect used oil
2. Disassemble the draining plug (1) and let dripping about 20 minutes
3. Clean the plug and lodging within the carter, allowing for new enclosing joint in the cap; do it and tighten to specified torque
4. Fill with recommended oil through hole (2) and check level using dipstick (3)
5. Put right oil amount for each case, start engine in neutral and check there are no leaks. After a few minutes, cut off engine, let oil become still and check with rod gauge (3) that oil mark is MAX; otherwise add oil in small quantities

**Oil Drain Plug Tightening Torque:** 3-4 Kg-m (30-40 Nm)

### Oil Filter Change

**Part Numbers:**
- Special spanner A: ref. 790972
- Spanner B: ref. 99360091

**Oil Filter Tightening Torque:** 2.5 Kg-m (25 Nm)

**Procedure:**
1. Use appropriate container to dispose used oil from filter
2. Using special spanner (A) ref. 790972, unlock the oil filter (4)
3. With appropriate clean new brush cleanse the area where filter contacts the coupling
4. Unwrap new filter from casing, apply engine oil in joints (5) and thread it by hand
5. Using special spanner (A) ref. 99360091, apply appropriate torque to filter
6. Start engine and check no oil leaks

**Caution:** Be sure that no hair rests, no dirt, remains in contact area of filter with coupling.

### Injectors Gauging (Item 1.4) - Mechanical Injection Only

**BOSCH Type Sprayer:** SACLESS DSLA 134 P 604

**Gauging Controls:**
- Below 200 bars: 230 ± 8 bar
- Running Gauging: 240 ± 12 bar

### Engine Coolant (Item 1.5)

**Total Circuit Capacity:** 12 litres

**Coolant Mixture:**
- Demineralized water: 50%
- Dinagel 9103 (Dinamic): 50%

**Freezing Protection:** -38°C

**Caution:** The coolant mixture should be revised in summer and in winter, as it works as an anti-freezer, refrigerant, corrosion protector and lubricating agent.

**Control:** Unlock cap (1) of expansion vase, check that refrigerant contains no less than 40% protector; otherwise substitute it.

**Inspection Procedures:**
1. With appropriate pressure pump check that cap (1) of tank holds pressure till 0.7 Kg (pressurizing circuit); otherwise replace cap
2. Check visually no leaks; worthiness of sleeves and tightness of braces
3. If mud or dirt in expansion vase, clean by disconnecting components, washing with pressurized water, appropriate detergent, rinsing well and reassembling
4. Proceed to fill as explained below, warm engine at running temperature and check no leaks

**Filling and Air Purging Procedure:**
1. Prepare coolant mixture as stated in table
2. Pour coolant slowly (approximately 8 litres per minute) through filling mouth (11) avoiding air pouches
3. Place screen in front radiator to help reach optimal working temperature
4. Start engine and keep it about 5 minutes at rate slightly above idle
5. If expansion tank empties totally in first minutes, switch off engine and complete filling
6. If liquid was withdrawn from circuit, air purging after filling is required
7. Speed up motor till 3,500 rpm to increase fast the temperature of refrigerant (94°C to 110°C)
8. Check for no leaks during air purging

**Note:** While air purging check there are no leaks; if there are, take appropriate steps to avoid them.

### Water Radiator and Intercooler (Item 1.6-1.7)

**External Cleaning:**
- Use pressurized water, compressed air and brush
- Do not use sharp objects that could damage refrigerating panel
- If necessary to remove intercooler, ease cleaning tasks by disconnecting rigid interconnecting pipe

### Air Filter (Item 1.8)

To purge accumulated residues on filter seat and for cleaning/substitution of filtering element, see Section 6C-48 "MANIPULATING THE AIR FILTER".

### Exhaust System (Item 1.9)

**Inspection:**
- Check worthiness of elastic supports (1)
- Check for leaks, significant dents, loose couplings or damages
- Check areas around exhaust system for damaged/badly fitted pieces
- Verify no frictions or rubbings with other pieces
- If there are defects, prompt action should be taken

**Wider Information:** See Section 6K-1 "EXHAUST SYSTEM"

### Visual Control (Item 1.10)

**Inspection:**
- Check worthiness of sleeves and pipes
- Check worthiness of braces and unions
- Replace pipes, sleeves or braces showing working deficiencies
- Replace if inadequate to avoid sudden failures

## Transmission Service

### Gear-Box Oil (Item 3.1)

**Recommended Oil:** SAE 75W-90, API GL-4
**Capacity:** 3.120 c.c.

**Note:** Use sealant TB 1215 QUIMILOCK for cap threads.

### Transfer Box Oil (Item 3.2)

**Recommended Oil:** SAE 75W-90, API GL-4
**Capacity:** 2.800 c.c.

**Note:** Use sealant TB 1215 QUIMILOCK for cap threads.

### Differential Groups Oil (Item 3.3)

**Recommended Oil:** SAE 80W-90, API GL-5 Hypoid Gears Oil
**Capacity:**
- Front Group: 1.750 c.c.
- Rear Group: 2.500 c.c.

**Note:** Use sealant TB 1215 QUIMILOCK for cap threads.

### Propeller Shafts (Item 3.4)

**Recommended Grease:** AGUILA 95 BRUGAROLAS

**Tightening Torque (Union Clamp Nuts):** 2.8 ± 3.5 kg-m (28 ± 35 Nm)

### Clutch Liquid (Item 3.5)

**Recommended:** DOT-4

**Caution:** Clutch liquid can damage car enamel. If liquid fell on enamel, clean well immediately.

| | Rear | 15.60 mm | 8.2 mm |
| Disc thickness | Front | 20 mm | 18 mm |
| | Rear | 10 mm | 9 mm |
| Disc deflection | | | 0.15 mm |

**Brake Liquid:** DOT-4

### Steering Mechanical System (Item 4.4)

**Steering Play (A):** 10 ± 30 mm

Check with vehicle standing, wheels straightened, engine off. Move steering wheel left and right gently noting when wheels start to move. This is the play (A).

### Servo-Steering Hydraulic Oil (Item 4.7)

**Recommended Hydraulic Oil:** DEXRON-II

**Oil Level Inspection:**
- Place vehicle on levelled ground with engine at idle
- Align wheels in straight position
- Take off filling cap (1), dismount filter (6)
- Adjust level if necessary:
  - Minimum: 6 cm
  - Maximum: 7 cm

**Note:** When engine stops, oil level will increase 1-2 cm

## Wheels and Tyres

### Wheel Nuts Tightening Torque (Item 4.10)

**Specification:** 11 ± 17 kg-m (110 ± 170 Nm)

**Tyre Rotation (Item 4.9):**
To equilibrate wear on tyres, exchange them periodically as shown in rotation pattern (4 wheels, radial tyres).

**Cautions:**
- Fitting new tyres must be done with equivalent to original ones
- Check tyres air pressure when cold
- Do not use tyres with different characteristics in same vehicle

## Final Inspection

Perform a road test to verify:
1. Engine starts smoothly
2. Clutch and gear shifts engage/disengage properly
3. Brakes function evenly without blockage
4. Parking brake holds on steep slopes at position 6 or 7
5. Engine responds quickly without abnormal vibrations
6. No unusual noises from body, wheels, transmission
7. Gauges and warning lights function properly
8. All body components secure and operating

---

## Safety Warnings

**WARNING:** All inspection works and changes must be carried out with ENGINE NOT RUNNING and BATTERY DISCONNECTED (if not necessary the electrical tension for the work been carried out).

**CAUTION:** When working with fluids and lubricants:
- Do not keep steering wheel at maximum position for more than 10 seconds
- Do not use sharp objects on cooling systems
- Ensure proper disposal of used oil and fluids
- Always use recommended specifications for replacements
