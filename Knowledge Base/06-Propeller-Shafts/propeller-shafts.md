# Section 4: Front and Rear Propeller Shafts

## Overview

This section covers the service procedures for the front drive shaft (4A), rear drive shaft (4B), and front/rear propeller shaft assembly (4C) of the Santana PS10 vehicle. These components form the power transmission system from the engine to the wheels.

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** To diagnose failures of the different components which could lead to abnormal behaviour of the general steering system, read carefully "CHARTS OF GENERAL DIAGNOSTICS" (3-2-2).

## Section 4A: Front Drive Shaft

### General Description

The front drive shaft system consists of multiple components that work together to transmit power from the transmission to the front wheels. The system includes:

**Major Components:**
1. King pin assembly
2. Oil-seal
3. King pin bearing
4. Pitman arm assembly
5. Steering knuckle
6. O-ring
7. King pin shim
8. Retainer (multiple)
9. Steering pivot and wheel hub assembly
10. Wheel spindle
11. Oil-seal
12. Steering pivot assembly
13. Differential group flange
14. Wheel hub assembly
15. Wheel spindle
16. Spindle bush
17. Shaft drive flange
18. Shaft
19. Circlip
20. Shim inner
21. Seadle bearing
22. Outer spindle bearing
23. Bearing
24. Retainer
25. Wheel center cap

### Service Components - Steering Pivot Assembly

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Place vehicle in appropriate ground to do this task
2. Remove front wheels (see 3F-4)
3. Remove brake caliper (1) of its connection to support (2) and take away together with sleeve

**WARNING - IMPORTANT NOTES:**
- Do not press brake lever till putting back again the brake clamp
- While doing this task, the brake clamp must be kept away adequately, do not allow it to be hanging from the brake sleeve

**Removal Steps (Continued):**

4. Remove fixing elements (3) of tie rod end joint (4) and disconnect it from its connection to the lower rotation axle cam. Use tool (A) ref 600590
5. Remove fixing elements (5) connecting flange (6) of the hemisphere and flange (7) of the axle housing
6. Remove steering pivot assembly (8) formed by the rotation axles, rotation axles casings, wheel hub, brake disc and drive shaft

**Installation Procedure:**

1. To carry out install of assemblies, reverse removal procedures noting remarks and tightening torques:
   - Inspect the worthiness of the oil-seal and substitute if damage, wear or deformations are observed
   - Ensure that faces of the couplings between the housing flange and hemisphere flange are clean
   - Apply grease to lip of oil-seal enclosure
   - Apply recommended sealant to the faces of the coupling between the housing flange and the hemisphere flange

2. **Fixing elements (5) connecting bridles tightening torque:**
   - 3.5 + 4.25 Kg-m (35 + 42.5 Nm)
   - NOTE: Install new self braking nuts in the flange unions

3. Tighten to the specified tightening torque nut fixing tie rod end joint and block it with pins

4. **Nuts fixing ball and socket joints tightening torque:**
   - 4 Kg-m (40 Nm)
   - Apply recommended sealant to nuts fixing brake caliper and tighten them to the specified tightening torque

5. **Tightening torque for screws fixing brake clamps to support:**
   - 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)
   - Tighten wheel nuts to the specified tightening torque following the steps sequence shown in the figure

6. **Tightening torque for wheel screws:**
   - 11 + 17 Kg-m (110 + 117 Nm)

### Removal and Installation Differential Group Assembly

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Unlock emptying plug (1) and remove oil from front differential group and assembly plug after applying the recommended sealant on the thread
2. Remove left and right steering pivot assemblies (see 4A-3)
3. Disconnect front transmission shaft (4) from its connection to differential group
4. Remove nuts (2) fixing group (3) to housing and remove differential group

**Installation Procedure:**

1. Apply recommended sealant to coupling face of the differential group (3) with the front axle housing
2. Assemble the group and fix it using nuts (2) to the specified tightening torque

**NOTE:** Install new self braking nuts

**Tightening torque for nuts fixing group:**
- 3.5 + 4.25 Kg-m (35 + 42.5 Nm)

3. Install front transmission shaft and fix it using its screws and screws (5) to the specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for transmission shaft fixing elements:**
- 2.8 + 3.5 Kg-m (28 + 35 Nm)

4. Install steering pivot assemblies (see 4A-3)
5. Fill with oil and level the front differential group (see 0B-17)

### Removal and Installation Front Drive Shaft Assembly

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Place the vehicle in a pit or an appropriate hydraulic lifter to carry out this task
2. Disconnect front propeller shaft from its connection to the differential group
3. Disconnect drive rod (1) from its connection to arm (2)
4. Remove shock absorber nuts (3) from connection to spring plates and disconnect shock absorber
5. Loose wheel nuts, lift front part of the vehicle, leaving both wheels suspended and the vehicle firmly anchored
6. Remove both front wheels
7. Remove screws (4) fixing brake caliper (5) and take it away together with the sleeve

**IMPORTANT WARNINGS:**
- Do not press brake lever till putting back again the brake caliper
- While doing this task, the brake caliper must be kept away adequately, do not allow it to be hanging from the brake sleeve

8. Remove U-bolt nuts (5) from both sides and withdraw plates (6)
9. Install both front wheels without tightening nuts and bring down the vehicle till the wheels rest on the ground and leaf springs become separated from the axle
10. Remove U-bolts
11. Loose all spring bolts, remove front bolts (7) and disconnect leaf springs from front connection to framework
12. Lift vehicle front part and place trestles at the framework (behind the wheels) to keep the vehicle in such position combined with the hydraulic lifting device

**WARNING:** Be sure that the vehicle stays firmly on the trestles and that they are firmly resting on the ground

13. Remove vehicle front drive shaft assembly, pulling from the front part and moving it with its own wheels

**Installation Procedure:**

1. To carry out front drive shaft assembly install, reverse removal procedures noting followings remarks and tightening torques

**IMPORTANT NOTES - With leaf springs under tension, move shaft housing to put in coincidence the screw head and its lodging within the axle housing:**
- Tighten nuts from bolts and bolts when the vehicle is fully resting on leaf springs

**Tightening torque of nuts and bolts fixing leaf springs to framework and shacle plates:**
- 9.5 Kg-m (95 Nm)

**Tightening torque of U-bolts nuts:**
- 7 Kg-m (70 Nm)

**Tightening torque for screws fixing brake caliper to support:**
- 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)

**Tightening torque of screws and nuts fixing the propeller shaft:**
- 2.8 + 3.5 Kg-m (28 + 35 Nm)

- Lighten wheel nuts following the steps sequence shown in the figure and apply the specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque:** 11 + 17 Kg-m (110 + 170 Nm)

### Oil-Seal of Differential Group Flange Substitution

1. Disconnect front propeller shaft (1) from connection to the differential group
2. Substitute the oil-seal following the same steps as indicated for the oil-seal of back differential group (see 4B-7)
3. Fill with oil till the correct level and connect propeller shaft fixing screws and nuts tightening them to the specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque:**
- Screws/nuts fixing transmission shaft: 2.8 + 3.5 Kg-m (28 + 35 Nm)
- Nut fixing engaging pinion flange: 11.75 Kg-m (117.5 Nm)

### Friction Pads Substitution

1. Remove wheel from the side you want to substitute the friction pads and firmly wedge the vehicle
2. Remove caliper (1) from its connection to support (2)
3. Remove friction pads (3) and replace them by new ones
4. Install caliper (1), lightening fixing screws to the specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for screws fixing clamp:**
- 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)

5. Install wheel and tighten nuts to the specified tightening torque following the sequence order shown in the figure

**Tightening torque for wheel nuts:**
- 11 + 17 Kg-m (110 + 117 Nm)

### Hemisphere Oil-Seals Substitution

1. Remove steering pivot assembly (see 4A-3)
2. Remove screws (1) fixing oil-seal extension case (2), withdraw oil-seal extension case and the oil-seal
3. Install a new oil-seal previously applying oil to the enclosing lip
4. Fix oil-seal to extension case (2) tightening screws to the specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for screws fixing oil-seal plate of the hemisphere oil-seal:**
- 0.8 + 1.1 Kg-m (8 + 11 Nm)

5. Install steering pivot assembly (see 4A-3)

### Front Shaft Housing Substitution

**Removal:**

1. Remove left and right steering pivot assemblies (see 4A-3)
2. Remove differential group (see 4A-5)
3. Remove U-bolts (1) and withdraw housing (2)

**Installation:**

1. Install a new housing and fix it with U-bolts tightening nuts to the specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for U-bolts nuts:**
- 7 Kg-m (70 Nm)

2. Install new oil-seals (3) on the housing and apply grease into the enclosing lips
3. Continue install the differential group and steering pivots as indicated in complementary task

### Steering Pivot and Wheel Hub Assembly

**General Description:**

The components, turning pivots, wheel hub, disc and axle shaft joint, form a unique ensemble installed over the hemisphere. The later is screwed to the front axle housing flange. With the exception of the hemisphere, all the components are mobiles (rotary). They are connected to the hemisphere through the two rotation axles; upper and lower. From the lower pivot, goes the drive cam to which the connecting rod between wheels and the steering rod drive (lower pivot of the right side front wheel) are connected.

**NOTE - REPAIR FLEXIBILITY:**
The repair can be done with the steering pivot ensemble fitted over the axle or, taking it away previously as an independent ensemble in order to repair it.

### Removal, Revision and Installation of the Steering Pivot and Wheel Hub Assembly

**Removal:**

1. Remove or disconnect the following components:
   - The o adequate wheel
   - Caliper
   - Tie rod end (see 4A-3)
   - Dismantle hub cap (1)

**Hub and flange removal:**

2. Remove oil-seal circlip (2) from rotating shaft and shim (3)
3. Remove fixing screws (4) from the drive shaft flange (5) and take it away

**Installation (Continued - Removal Phase):**

4. Straighten wings of catching washer (7)
5. Remove bushing nut and counter nut (6) using key ref. no. 161950, catching washers (7) and (8)
6. Withdraw wheel hub and disc (9)

**Removal hemisphere and housing lodging rotation shaft:**

7. Remove wheel spindle (10) and after the drive shaft remove (11)
8. Remove screws (12) fixing the oil-seal to extension case (13), take away extension case and oil-seal (14)
9. Straighten screw blocking plates (15), remove screws and withdraw upper rotation axle (16) with the adjustment plates; the upper bearing will remain lodged into the hemisphere
10. Take away housing (17) lodging rotation axles, joined to the rotation axle/lower arm (19) and bearing (18)

## Important Safety Cautions

### General Warnings

- Never use heat to loosen blocked wheel lug nuts; wheel life time could be shortened
- Always support the vehicle securely on trestles when working underneath
- Never press brake lever when brake caliper is removed
- Keep brake caliper supported adequately during service
- Ensure new self-braking nuts are installed in flange unions

### Service Precautions

- Inspect oil-seals for wear or deformation and replace if necessary
- Ensure coupling faces are clean before reassembly
- Apply recommended sealant to coupling faces and oil-seal lips
- Never allow drive shaft to hang from brake sleeve during service
- Verify all fasteners are tightened to specification in proper sequence

### Vehicle Safety During Service

- Place vehicle in pit or on hydraulic lifter for drive shaft removal
- Use appropriate lifting and supporting equipment
- Never work under vehicle supported only by jack
- Keep work area clear of obstructions
- Wear safety glasses and appropriate personal protective equipment
