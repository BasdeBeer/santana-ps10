## GENERAL DIAGNOSIS

### Diagnostic Procedure Overview
(Service Manual, Page 3-2)

When diagnosing steering, suspension, wheels and tires issues, follow this procedure:

1. **Inspect tires** for proper pressure and uneven wear
2. **Raise vehicle** on hoist and inspect front and rear suspension and rack/pinion
3. **Spin front wheels** and inspect for:
   - Tire runout
   - Wheel runout
   - Bent rims
   - Rough wheel bearings
4. **Ensure all tires** are of the same type and of the same treadmark

### Toe Setting Procedure
(Service Manual, Page 3A-4)

**Check Method:**
1. Place the vehicle on levelled ground with front wheels along the vehicle length
2. Measure the actual length (B) at height (A) with appropriate meter and signalize on the tyre the measuring points
3. Displace the vehicle towards the back so the wheels rotate 180° and proceed to measure length (C) using the same measuring points with the appropriate meter
4. Subtract lengths (C)-(B), the result value must be somewhere between the maximum and the minimum specified values:
   - Toe value: Minimum 2 mm, Maximum 4 mm (Service Manual, Page 3A-4)

**Adjustment Procedure:**
1. Loosen the counter nuts (1) which fix tie-rod ends (2) to the tie rod (3) connecting the wheels
2. Turn the tie rod (3) upwards to open the wheel front parts and downwards to close them; correcting thus simultaneously lengths (B) and (C). Repeat this operation until the toe reaches a value between the maximum and minimum limits.
3. Once adjusted tighten nuts (1)
4. Move the vehicle, to and fro, a few meters, while moving the steering wheel to settle the tie-rod ends and check again for toe
5. **Balance accordingly the steering wheel rotation towards the right and towards the left acting on the steering rod (4) and put right the steering wheel again if necessary (see 3C-4)**

### Checking Alignment: Camber, Caster and Kingpin Inclination
(Service Manual, Page 3A-5)

**Preliminary Checks Prior to Adjusting Alignment:**

Before making any alignment adjustment, perform these checks (Service Manual, Page 3A-3):

1. Check all tires for proper inflation pressures and approximately the same tread wear
2. Check for loose ball joints; excessive looseness noted must be corrected before adjusting
3. Check for run-out of wheels and tires
4. Check vehicle trim heights; if out of limits, correction must be made before adjusting toe
5. Check for loose suspension arms
6. Excess loads must be removed before alignment checks
7. Consider condition of equipment being used
8. Vehicle must be on levelled surface both fore and aft and transversely

**Alignment Specifications:**

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Camber | 1° 30' |
| Caster | 3° |
| Kingpin inclination | 7° |

**Note:** Should camber or caster be found out of specification upon inspection, locate its cause first. If it is in damaged, loose, bent, dented or worn suspension parts, they should be replaced. If it is in vehicle body, perapir it so as to attain specifications. (Service Manual, Page 3A-5)

### Turning Radius
(Service Manual, Page 3A-5)

**Turning radius: 7.3 m**

**Note:** When the tie rod (1) or its tie rod end have been substituted, be respectful with the original distances (A) between centres of the tie rods end and assemble original tie rods end respecting the distance (A). If the steering wheel is badly positioned, put it back correctly as indicated in 3C-4. (Service Manual, Page 3A-5)

## STEERING GEAR BOX AND LINKAGE

### Power Steering System Description
(Service Manual, Page 3B-3)

Power steering system reduces driver tiredness to rotate the steering wheel, using the hydraulic pressure generated by the power steering pump.

The mechanical gear unit, the hydraulic pressure cylinder unit and the control valve unit constitute an integral set, and they are found in the gear box.

**System Components:**
1. Power steering pump
2. Power steering gear box (mechanical gear, hydraulic cylinder, control valve)
3. Oil tank
4. Hydraulic hoses and fittings

### Power Steering Gear Box
(Service Manual, Page 3B-7)

#### Removal and Installation

**Caution:** Before starting removal clean carefully the components to be dismantled and the surroundings. (Service Manual, Page 3B-7)

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Place the vehicle in an appropriate working place
2. Place wheels straightened, which is equivalent to have the steering at the middle of its left or right handling
3. Remove left front wheel (see 3F-4)
4. Disconnect steering lower shaft (7) from its connection to the steering gear box (6)
5. Disconnect from steering gear box the pressure hose (1) and the return hose (2), beforehand, place under it an appropriate container to drip the liquid at the hydraulic circuit
6. Remove power steering liquid tank (3)
7. Take away clamp (4) of the tank
8. Remove the generator (see 6A-52)

**Caution:** Protect with appropriate caps the openings which remain opened and the hoses, intake of foreign bodies trough these openings could seriously damage the servo steering. (Service Manual, Page 3B-7)

9. Dismantle nuts (5) and remove the power steering gear box unit (6)

**Installation:**

1. Install power steering gear box (6) fixing nuts (5) at the specified torque:
   - **Tightening torque for fixing nuts of the power steering gear box: 7 ± 10 Kg-m (70 ± 100 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3B-8)
2. Install generator (see 6A-52)
3. Install power steering liquid tank (3)
4. Connect pressure hose (1) and return hose (2):
   - **Tightening torque: Pressure inlet connector (1) to the power steering gear box: 4 ± 5 Kg-m (40 ± 50 Nm)**
   - **Tightening torque: Oil return connector (2) to the tank: 3 ± 4 Kg-m (30 ± 40 Nm)**
   - **Tightening torque: Nuts fixing joints to cams: 4 Kg-m (40 Nm)**
5. Install steering lower shaft and fix its nut to the specified torque:
   - **Tightening torque nut connecting shaft to the power steering gear box axle: 2 ± 3 Kg-m (20 ± 30 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3B-8)
6. Install left front wheel, lower the vehicle and follow the sequence shown in the figure to tighten fixing nuts to the specified torque:
   - **Tightening torque for nuts fixing wheels: 11 ± 17 Kg-m (110 ± 170 Nm)**
7. Carry out the filling and purging of the servo steering hydraulic circuit (see OB-26 and 3B-12)
8. Check if the steering wheel positioning rightwards and leftwards is balanced; if it is not, adjust it adequately and relocate the steering wheel if necessary (see 3C-4)

### On Vehicle Service - Steering Wheel and Components

#### Steering Wheel Inspection
(Service Manual, Page 3B-10)

1. With the engine off, wheels straightened and resting on the ground, make small turns with the steering wheel both sides. Annotate the play or dead path (A) that the steering wheel do before the wheels move.
2. Check that the measured play lies between the allowed values; if this is not so, locate the component with excessive play and repair.

**Steering wheel play (A) of the:** 10 ± 30 mm (Service Manual, Page 3B-10)

3. Check for looseness or rattling of the steering wheel by moving it in the axle direction and sideways.

#### Power Steering Force Inspection
(Service Manual, Page 3B-10)

1. Put the vehicle on a levelled place and the steering wheel in the straightforward direction
2. Check if tyres pressure is correct
3. Start engine and leave idle lower steering oil reach 50 to 60 °C temperature. If it is necessary, turn steering wheel both sides to increase quickly the hydraulic oil temperature.
4. With engine running at idling speed, measure with a spring balancer, as illustrated in figure, the forced needed to action the power steering which must be below 5 Kg.

**Servo steering handling force: < 5 Kg** (Service Manual, Page 3B-10)

#### Power Steering Fluid Leakage Inspection
(Service Manual, Page 3B-11)

1. Start engine and turn completely the steering wheel in both directions to get the maximum hydraulic pressure
2. Inspect visually if there are any leaks in the hydraulic pump, the liquid tank, hose connections or power steering gear box

**Caution:** Do not keep the steering wheel turned at the maximum position for more than 10 seconds. (Service Manual, Page 3B-11)

#### Purging and Substitution of the Steering Liquid
(Service Manual, Page 3B-12)

1. Lift the front part of the vehicle and put an appropriate trestle to keep wheels above ground
2. Disconnect the oil return hose from tank
3. Turn steering wheel leftwards and run the engine, a maximum of 10 seconds, to evacuate the oil from pump
4. Stop engine and turn fully to and fro the steering wheel to extract all the oil from the circuit
5. Connect the oil return hose to the tank and fill again, till the specified level (see OB-26)

**Note:** Always use new, fresh, recommended hydraulic oil. (Service Manual, Page 3B-12)

6. Start the engine, and turn the steering wheel several times fully in both directions till and add oil till the specified level
7. Check there are no oil leaks through linkages or connections
8. Install the tank cap, close the hood, bring down the vehicle from the trestle and try it

**Caution:** Do not keep the steering wheel turned at the maximum position for more than 10 seconds. (Service Manual, Page 3B-12)

**Note:** Uncompleted air purging will entail froth within the tank and harsh sounds when turning the steering wheel. (Service Manual, Page 3B-12)

#### Hydraulic Pressure Inspection
(Service Manual, Page 3B-13)

**Back Pressure Check:**

1. With the wheels straightened (steering at the middle of its left or right course), let the engine to idle speed check that the hydraulic pressure corresponds to the specified one:
   - **Back pressure: < than 10 Kg/cm²** (Service Manual, Page 3B-13)
2. If higher than the indicated value, check for possible blockages in the control valve or in pipes

**Relief Pressure Check (Checking at pump):**

7. Increase engine speed till about 1500 rpm
8. Close slowly the manometer valve while reading with it the pressure increase
9. Note the maximum pressure value reading, which must be in accordance with the value indicated below:
   - **Maximum Pressure: 60 ± 80 Kg/cm²** (Service Manual, Page 3B-13)

**Caution:** Be sure of not having manometer valve closed more than 10 seconds. (Service Manual, Page 3B-13)

**Note:** If checks to determine the worthiness of the hydraulic pump have been successful, check worthiness of the power steering gear box as explained below.

**Maximum pressure in the power steering gear box (Checking at gear box):**

11. Open completely the manometer valve and increase the engine speed till about 1500 rpm
12. Turn fully the steering wheel leftwards and rightwards. The maximum pressure should be the stated value:
    - **Maximum pressure: 60 ± 80 Kg/cm²** (Service Manual, Page 3B-14)
13. If the reading is lower, probably there is a failure in the mechanism of the power steering gear box. Substitute box.

**Caution:** Do not keep the steering wheel turned at the maximum position for more than 10 seconds. (Service Manual, Page 3B-14)

## STEERING WHEEL, COLUMN AND LOWER SHAFT

### General Description
(Service Manual, Page 3C-2)

The steering column can be easily dismantled or reassembled. To ensure the adequate energy damping, it is important to use only the original SANTANA fixing elements, and tightening up to the specified torques. When dismantling the vehicle steering ensemble, attention must be paid to carefully handling. The eventual sharp hitting at the end of the axle ensemble or the accidental falling of such ensemble could lead to the breaking of the security plastic pins holding the steering column and lower shaft.

**Components:**
1. Steering wheel
2. Steering wheel cover
3. Steering column assembly
4. Steering lower shaft
5. Steering column upper cover
6. Steering column lower cover

### On Vehicle Service

#### Steering Wheel Removal and Installation
(Service Manual, Page 3C-3)

**Removal:**

1. Disconnect negative battery cable
2. Removal steering wheel cover (1) and nut (2) from the steering column
3. Do coincidental marks (A) on the steering wheel and the steering column, to use them as a guide to assembly:
   - **Note:** Such coincidental marks (A), are necessary only in the case the steering wheel is going to be dismantled and put back again. (Service Manual, Page 3C-3)
4. Remove steering wheel using an appropriate extractor

**Installation:**

1. Install steering wheel over the column aligning the coincidental marks (A) which you when dismantling
2. Tighten nut (2) from steering column to the specified torque:
   - **Tightening torque for the fixing nut of the steering wheel: 2,5 ± 4 Kg-m (25 ± 40 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3C-3)
3. Install steering wheel cover (1) and connect back the battery
4. Run straightforward the vehicle for a short distance, to confirm the correct positioning of the steering wheel

#### Steering Wheel Repositioning
(Service Manual, Page 3C-4)

1. With the wheels straightened, which is equivalent to keep the steering at the centre of possible leftwards and rightwards turning positions, check if the steering wheel stay in the adequate position; otherwise, proceed to reposition the steering wheel

**Steering wheel repositioning:**

2. With wheels straightened as indicated above, dismantle the steering wheel and adjust it in the correct position without tightening nut (2) completely
3. Run straightforward the vehicle for a short distance, to confirm the correct positioning of the steering wheel. If the trial is satisfactory, tighten fixing nut (2) to the tightening torque below specified and assemble cover (1). If during the trial you observe some imbalance, correct the steering wheel position as required.

**Tightening torque of fixing nut of the steering wheel:** 2,5 ± 4 Kg-m (25 ± 40 Nm) (Service Manual, Page 3C-4)

#### Ignition Switch and Steering Lock Assembly

**Removal:**

1. Remove steering wheel (see 3C-3)
2. Remove combination switch and wind screen wiper (see 3C-5)
3. Disconnect coupler (1) from the instrument panel wiring connector
4. Remove slave screw (2) fixing the ignition switch assembly (3) to the steering column

**Installation:**

3. Turn ignition switch to position (0) and extract it, in this form the steering lock assembly is activated
4. Align hub of the steering lock assembly and turn the steering shaft to check it get properly engaged
5. Fix the ignition switch to assembly (3) using slave screw (2), tightening it to the specified torque:
   - **Tightening torque of slave screw: 1,3 ± 1,5 Kg-m (13 ± 15 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3C-7)
6. Connect coupler (1) to the instrument panel wiring connector
7. Assemble the other components reversing the dismantling steps and noting the specified tightening torques

#### Steering Lower Shaft

**Removal:**

5. Install upper fixing support (1) of steering lower shaft to the body and fix it with the appropriate screws tightened to the specified torque:
   - **Tightening torque of upper fixing screws of the steering lower shaft: 1,8 ± 2,8 Kg-m (18 ± 28 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3C-9)
6. Assemble the rest of the components reversing the dismantling order, noting the specified tightening torques

(Service Manual, Page 3D-2)

**Component Identification:**

1. Leaf spring
2. Spring bush
3. Spring bumper
4. Snackle bush
5. Spring seat
6. Shackle inner plate
7. Shackle outer plate
8. U bolt
9. Spring plate bracket
10. Shock absorber

**System Features:**

The PS10 front suspension uses semi-elliptical leaf springs with integral shock absorbers integrated into the suspension geometry.

### On Vehicle Service

#### Leaf Springs and Shock Absorbers
(Service Manual, Page 3D-3)

**Dampers Removal:**

1. Remove nut (1) and washer (2) fixing shock absorber (3)
2. Release silent blocks (4) from shock absorber

3. Remove from the lower part of shock absorber:
   - nut (5)
   - washer (6)
4. Release shock absorber from its connection to spring seat as well as the silent blocks (7)

**Leaf Springs Removal:**

5. Install a hydraulic lifter device under the front part of the vehicle and lift it till front wheel is loose
6. Remove nuts (8) from "U" bolt (9) and withdraw fixing spring seat (10)
7. Remove bolts counter nuts (11) which ensure the bolts tightening on shackle plates and bring down the vehicle till the wheels rest on the ground and springs lose contact with axle
8. Loosen screws (11) connecting shackle plates to leaf spring and lean the back end of spring on the ground
9. Loosen them to remove the one connecting the shackle plates to leaf spring and lean the back end of spring on the ground

**WARNING:** Secure the vehicle with trestles in area which that not interfere with the spring extraction. (Service Manual, Page 3D-3)

**Components Revision:**

11. Check if remove components are sound and they show no wear neither breaking; substituting them when necessary

**Leaf Springs Installation:**

12. Connect leaf spring front part to body using screw (14) and nut (13), and the rear of leaf spring to shackle plates (12) with screw (11), do not fully tighten nuts and bolts.
13. Lift the vehicle till the axle rests suspended from the springs and centre it till the spring top (15) is perfectly lodged in the axle supporting point (16)
14. Install "U" bolts (9), spring seats (10) and nuts (8):
    - **Tightening torque for nuts fixing U bolts: 7 Kg-m (70 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3D-5)
15. Bring down the vehicle till wheels rest on the ground and take away the hydraulic lifting device
16. Tighten to specified torque bolts and nuts fixing spring front and back ends:
    - **Tightening torque for bolts and nuts: 9,5 Kg-m (95 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3D-5)

**Dampers Installation:**

18. Fix the upper of shock absorber (3) with silent blocks (4) and top washes (2), install nut (1) without tightening
19. Fix the lower of the shock absorber (3) like its upper
20. Tighten nuts till reaching the top

### Springs Bush and Body
(Service Manual, Page 3D-6)

1. Remove shock absorber and front leaf springs (see 3D-3)
2. Check silent blocks worthiness or if they have pittings, breakings, deformations, etc.
3. Substitute the bad silenblock using the special hydraulic tool to extract and install the silent blocks
4. Install the other components reversing the install order and taking into account the specified tightening torques

### Spring Bumper
(Service Manual, Page 3D-6)

1. If it is necessary to substitute some spring bumper, remove fixing screws (1) and withdraw top
2. Check that tops are not unstuck, cracked, deformed, etc; otherwise, change ensemble for another new one
3. Install spring bumper ensemble with its fixing screws

### Front Suspension Inspection
(Service Manual, Page 3D-7)

1. Remove shock absorbers and front leaf springs (see 3D-3)
2. Place shock absorber (1) in a vice, fixing its lower part
3. Compress and extend the shock absorber
4. Check if hardness along each course is constant
5. If hardness is intermittent or weak, substitute shock absorber for a new one

**NOTE:** The shock absorber hardness is different in each part of the course, being higher in the extending course. (Service Manual, Page 3D-7)

6. Check that leaf spring free height is as the specified one:
   - **Front leaf spring free height: 120 mm** (Service Manual, Page 3D-7)
7. If leaf spring free height is lower than this value, check if leaf spring is sound and there are no breakings neither wear. If it is sound then try to get back the right value sending it to a qualified centre, otherwise replace it for a new one
8. Proceed to reassemble the components reversing the remove order and taking into account the specified tightening torques

**System Features:**

The PS10 rear suspension uses semi-elliptical leaf springs with shock absorbers. The design is similar to the front suspension but configured for rear axle support.

### On Vehicle Service

#### Shock Absorbers and Leaf Springs
(Service Manual, Page 3E-3)

**Shock Absorbers Removal:**

1. Remove nut (1) and top washers (2) fixing the shock absorber (3) to the upper part
2. Take away shock absorber (3) and silentblocks (4)
3. Remove nut and counter nut (5) fixing shock absorber (3) to rear shaft housing, remove washers (6) and rubber (7)
4. Remove shock absorber (3)

**Leaf Springs Removal:**

5. Remove leaf springs as same front leaf springs (see 3D-3)

**Components Check:**

6. Check if the removed components are in good condition and do not show wear or breakage. If found defective, replace

**Leaf Spring Installation:**

7. Reverse removal procedures, noting tighten to specified torque (see 3D-3):
   - **Tightening torque for nut fixing the shock absorber: 4 ± 6 Kg-m (40 ± 60 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3E-4)

**Shock Absorber Installation:**

8. Install shock absorber (3) on right position, install in the lower part the washers (6) and the rubber (7)
9. Install nut and counter nut (5) fixing shock absorber to rear shaft housing and tighten to specified torque:
   - **Tightening torque for nut fixing the shock absorber: 4 ± 6 Kg-m (40 ± 60 Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3E-4)
10. Install bumpers (4) on.
11. Fix shock absorber (3) and washers (2) to the upper part, tightening nut (1)

## WHEELS AND TIRES

### General Description
(Service Manual, Page 3F-2)

**TIRES:**

Concerning tires size to be used in this vehicle, see the OWNER'S MANUAL. The tyres are of tubeless type. They are designed to operate satisfactorily with loads up to the full rated load capacity when inflated to the recommended inflation pressure.

Correct tire pressures and driving habits have an important influence on tire life heavily corning, excessively rapid acceleration, and unnecessary sharp braking increase tire wear.

**REPLACEMENT TIRES:**

When replacement in ecessary, the original equipment type tire should be used. Refer to the Tire Placard. Replacement tires should be of the same size, load range and construction as those originally on the vehicle. Use of any other size or type tire may affect ride, handling, speedometer/odometer calibration, vehicle ground clearance and tire or snow chain clearance to the body and chassis.

**NOTE:** Do not mix different types of tires on the same vehicle such as radial, bras-belted tires except in emergencies, because vehicle handling may be seriously affected and may result in loss of control.

It is recommended that new tires be installed in pairs on the same axle. If necessary to replace only one tire, it should be paired with the tire having the must tread, to equalize braking traction. (Service Manual, Page 3F-2)

**WHEELS REPLACEMENT:**

Wheels must be replaced if they are bent, dented, have excessive lateral or radial renout, leak air through welds, have abogated bolt holes, if lug nuts won't staid tight, or if they are heavily rusted.

Wheels for replacement must be equivalent to the originally equipped wheels in load capacity, diameter, rim width, off-set and mounting configuration. A wheel of improper size or type may affect wheel and bearing life, breaking cooling, speedometer/odometer, calibration, ground clearance to the body and chassis. (Service Manual, Page 3F-2)

### Maintenance and Minor Adjustments

#### Wheels Maintenance
(Service Manual, Page 3F-3)

**Wheels Maintenance:**

Wheel repairs that use welding, heating or prening are not approved. All damaged wheels should be replaced.

**Tires Inflating:**

The pressure recommended has been carefully calculated to get the best performance in what concerns running comfort, stability, steering, tread wear, tires life and resistance to bruises.

Tire pressure must be checked monthly or before a long drive, when the tires are cool (after three or more hours standing of the vehicle, or having run less than 2 Km). Keep to the specified inflating pressures to be found in the Owner's Manual.

It is normal for tire pressure increase when the tires become hot during driving. Do not bleed or reduce tire pressure after driving. Bleeding reduces the «Cold Inflation Pressure».

**Higher than recommended pressure can cause:**
1. Hard ride
2. Tire bruising or carcass damage
3. Rapid tread wear at center of tire

**Lower than recommended pressure can cause:**
1. Tire squeal on turns
2. Hard steering
3. Rapid end uneven wear on the edges of the tread
4. Tire rim bruises and rupture
5. High tire temperatures
6. Reduce handling
7. High fuel consumption
8. Tire cord breakage

**Unequal pressure on same axle can cause:**
1. Uneven braking
2. Steering lead
3. Reduced handling
4. Swervie on acceleration

Valve caps should be kept on valves tro keep dust and water out. (Service Manual, Page 3F-3)

**Tires Rotation:**

To balance tire wear interchange periodically the tires as shown. (Service Manual, Page 3F-3)

### On Vehicle Service

#### Metric Lug Nuts and Wheels Studs
(Service Manual, Page 3F-4)

The vehicle use metric lug nuts and wheels studs M12x1,25.

**NOTE:** Always a wheel is remove, check worthiness of lug nuts and wheels studs; substitute when necessary. (Service Manual, Page 3F-4)

#### Wheels Removal and Installation
(Service Manual, Page 3F-4)

**Removal:**

1. Loose wheel nuts about 180° (half a rotation)
2. Lift the vehicle from the side involved and wedge it
3. Remove wheel

**CAUTION:** Never use heat to loosen blocked wheel lug nuts, because the wheel life time could be shortened and induced damages in the components. (Service Manual, Page 3F-4)

**Installation:**

1. Reverse removal procedures and tighten nuts to specified torque; follow the tightening order shown in figure to avoid bending of wheels or brake discs:
   - **Tightening torque of wheel fixing nuts: 11±17 Kg-m (110±170Nm)** (Service Manual, Page 3F-4)

**NOTE:** Before installing wheels, remove eventual corrosion at the assembly surface and brake disc, with a steel wire brush. Installing the wheels without a good metal to metal contact an mounting surfaces can cause wheel nuts to loosen, which can later allow wheel to come off while vehicle is moving. (Service Manual, Page 3F-4)

### Tires Substitution and Repair
(Service Manual, Page 3F-5)

**Removal:**

1. Remove wheel (see 3F-4)
2. Remove tire from rim

**NOTE:** To carry out the assembly or dismantling of the tire you must use an appropriate machine, and follow the manufacturer instructions. Do no use hand tools or levers to change the tires, because the tire and the wheel could be damaged. (Service Manual, Page 3F-5)

**Repair:**

1. Made the tyre repair according to needs or proceed to substitute it

**NOTE:** There are many different materials and techniques on the market to repair tire. As not all of these work on all types of tires, tire manufacturers have published detailed instructions or how and when to repair tires. These instructions can be obtained from each tire manufacturer. (Service Manual, Page 3F-5)

**Installation:**

1. Install the wheel (see 3F-4), noting the specified tightening torques and the tightening order:

**NOTE:** The seats of the tyres on the rim, must be adequately cleaned with a wire brush or rough steel wool to remove lubricant, rubber remainders and rust. Before install or removing a tyre, adequate lubrication should be applied to the tyre seats. (Service Manual, Page 3F-5)

2. After install, inflate the tires at 2,4 Kg/cm² so that beads are completely seated:

**WARNING:** Do not stand over tire when inflating. Bead may break when bead snaps over rim's safety hump and cause serious personal injury.
Do not exceed 2,4 kg/cm² inflating pressure will not seat beads, deflate, re-lubricate and reinflate. Over inflation may cause bead to break and cause serious personal injury. (Service Manual, Page 3F-5)

### Wheels Balancing
(Service Manual, Page 3F-6)

**Balancing Tipes:**

There are two balancing modes of wheels and tires: static and dynamic. The static balancing, is the equal distribution weight around the wheel.

Statically unbalanced wheels lead to bouncing described as wandering about, This will cause eventually uneven tires wear.

Dynamic balancing, is the uniform distribution of on each side of the while center line, in such a way that tires turnings does not imply lateral movement tendency. Dynamically unbalanced wheels could lead to shimmy.

**NOTE:** If any type of wheels unbalance is observed bring the vehicle to a specialized centre. (Service Manual, Page 3F-6)

## NOTES AND WARNINGS

### General Safety Precautions
(Service Manual, Section 3, General)

- **All fasteners play fundamental role in system performance.** Use only replacement parts with correct part numbers or equivalent.
- **Do not use substitute parts of lesser quality.** During reassembly, strictly apply specified tightening torques.
- **Never attempt to heat, quench or straighten suspension parts.** Replace damaged parts with new ones only.
- **Wheels and suspension fasteners are critical.** Ensure proper torque specification to prevent loosening and vehicle damage.
- **Power steering system pressures are critical.** Do not exceed specified pressures during testing.
- **Steering wheel turning at maximum positions.** Do not hold steering wheel at maximum turn for more than 10 seconds when engine is running, as this can damage the power steering system.
- **Air in power steering circuit.** Incomplete air purging will entail froth within the tank and harsh sounds when turning the steering wheel.

### Service Notes

- The PS10 uses a **power-assisted steering system** with hydraulic pressure generated by a vane pump.
- **Front suspension** uses semi-elliptical leaf springs with integral shock absorbers.
- **Rear suspension** is similar to front suspension but with different free height specifications.
- **Wheel alignment** should be checked after any suspension work or accident impact.
- **Tire pressure** should be checked when tires are cold (more than 3 hours standing or less than 2 km driving).
- **Wheels must never be welded, heated, or prened.** Damaged wheels must be replaced.
