# Steering, Suspension, Wheels and Tires Overview (Section 3)

**Source:** Santana PS10 Service Manual (791044-C1I), Pages 52-150

## System Architecture Overview

The Santana PS10 features integrated steering and suspension systems with power-assisted steering and independent front suspension with leaf springs at the rear. This document provides an overview; detailed subsections are referenced for specific components.

---

## General Diagnosis

### Comprehensive Diagnosis Approach

When diagnosing problems in steering, suspension, wheels and tires:

1. **Inspect tires** for proper pressure and uneven wear
2. **Raise vehicle** on hoist and inspect front and rear suspension and rack/pinion
3. **Spin front wheels** and inspect for runout of tires, auto-off-balance, bent rims, and rough wheel bearings
4. **Ensure all tires** are of the same type and of the same treadmark

### Section 3A: Front End Alignment

**Alignment Service Data (Unloaded):**
- **Toe-in:** 2 to 4 mm
- **Camber:** 1° 30'
- **Caster:** 3°
- **Kingpin indication:** 7°

**Alignment Adjustment Procedures:**
- Preliminary checks prior to adjusting alignment
- Toe setting procedure
- Camber, caster and kingpin inclination checks
- Turning radius measurement (7.3 m)

**Key Check Points Before Alignment:**
1. All tires properly inflated and approximately same tread wear
2. Check for loose ball joints; excessive looseness noted must be corrected before adjusting
3. Check for run-out of wheels and tires
4. Check vehicle trim heights; if out of limits, correction must be made before adjusting toe
5. Check for loose suspension arms
6. Excess loads must be removed before alignment checks
7. Consider condition of equipment being used
8. Vehicle must be on levelled surface both fore and aft and transversely

## Tire Diagnosis Guide

### Irregular and/or Premature Wear

**Causes:**
- Hard cornering
- Under inflation
- Lack of tire rotation
- Incorrect wheel alignment
- Tire construction non-uniform
- Wheel heavy acceleration

**Correction:** Wheel alignment check required for abnormal wear patterns.

### Radial Tire Waddle

**Definition:** Side-to-side movement felt at low speed (10-50 km/h) or as pushing on vehicle side when straight on level road.

**Diagnosis:** Use Tire Problem Detector (TPD) if available. Alternative method involves substituting known good tires and testing.

**Procedure:**
1. Ride vehicle to determine front or rear waddle
2. Install known good tires on waddling end
3. Road test again; if waddle end cannot be identified, substitute rear tires
4. Road test once more; if no improvement noted, reinstall originals same as above

### Radial Tire Lead

**Definition:** Vehicle dragging to one side on level road without steering wheel pressure.

**Cause:** Tire belt not straight within tire causing lateral forces when driving straight.

**Correction:** Front alignment precision check may be required; however, worn or improperly manufactured tires must be substituted with known good tire/wheel assemblies.

### Vibration Diagnosis

**Most common causes of highway speed vibration:**
1. Tire runout
2. Wheel runout
3. Tire stiffness variation

**Measurement Methods:**
Using Tire Problem Detector (TPD) will uncover only part of problem. Loaded radial runout must be checked using TPD and following equipment manufacturer recommendations.

If TPD unavailable, alternative method of substituting known good tire/wheel assemblies on problem vehicle can be used (though longer process).

## Important Notes

### Safety Considerations

- All fasteners play fundamental role in system performance
- Use only replacement parts with correct part numbers or equivalent
- Do not use substitute parts of lesser quality
- During reassembly, strictly apply specified tightening torques
- Never attempt to heat, quench or straighten suspension parts
- Replace damaged parts with new ones only

### Diagnostic Methodology

- Always perform preliminary inspection before making adjustments
- Ensure vehicle is on level surface
- Check tires first for pressure and wear patterns
- Perform road test before and after any adjustment
- Document findings and corrective actions taken
