# Santana PS10 Body Service

**Source:** Service Manual PS10, Section 9 (Pages 9-1 through 9-33)

---

## Section Overview

Complete body service procedures for the Santana PS10, including doors, windows, glass, locks, seals, seats, frame, and anticorrosion treatment.

---

## Front and Rear Doors

### Door Glass

#### Removal - Page 9-2

**Trim Removal:**
1. Remove door regulator handle (1)
2. Remove handle case (2)
3. Remove pull handle (3)
4. Remove inside lock knob (4)
5. Remove trim screws (5)
6. Remove interior plastic part from door

**Glass Removal - Page 9-2:**
1. Remove screws fixing regulator support plate and fixing guide to mechanism
2. Withdraw one of the glass guiding profiles to leave it free
3. Withdraw glass through the lower part and its guiding channel
4. Separate glass from channel

**Installation - Page 9-2 to 9-3:**
1. To fix glass (6) in the lower guiding channel (7), apply soapy water on the guide and fit the glass using a plastic hammer
2. Dimensions: Width A = Width B (adjust for even spacing)
3. If glass does not lift or lower easily, check that glass is inclined towards the door frame
4. Adjust window run regulator so dimensions A and B are the same
5. Put glass in the lower guide as shown
6. If glass difficulty is encountered, it could be that the glass is inclined towards the door frame
7. Adjust the window run regulator in such a way that dimensions A and B are the same
8. Secure sealing door cover with appropriate adhesive

## Back Door

### Door Glass and Lateral Window - Page 9-7 to 9-9

#### Removal
1. Clean around the internal and external sides of glass
2. Remove spare wheel
3. Stick band at body, around the glass, to avoid possible damages when extracting and assembling the glass
4. Disconnect defrosting electrical heating pad
5. Remove expanding strip (1)

**Glass Removal - Page 9-8:**
1. Using an appropriate punch, remove weather strip (2) and glass (3)

**Installation - Page 9-8 to 9-9:**

**NOTE:** If the weather strip to be fixed has been provided as a roll, slice a piece having the glass perimeter plus a 25 mm excess in order to be sure there will be a proper fitting of the glass when installed.
Do the cutting at right angles with some sort of fretting saw.

1. Fit weather strip to the frame border noting to the curved areas coupling and press the weather strip till any leftovers made while cutting it get properly fitted
2. Apply soapy water to the glass border and put it on the weather strip and assembly the strip lip progressively onto the glass using an appropriate punch
3. Once the weather strip lip has been assembled on glass, press firmly glass onto the weather strip

**Installation of the Expanding Strip - Page 9-9:**
1. Take a roll of expanding strip (4), cut one end at right angle and start assembly the strip tip beginning now at the opposite lip to the weather strip joint
2. To assemble expanding strip use tool (A) ref. 262771
3. Once done, cut at right angle the expanding strip leaving 6 mm more and pressing till proper fitting

## Windscreen - Page 9-12 to 9-15

#### Removal - Page 9-12
1. Remove windscreen arms (1)
2. Remove screws fixing windscreen to roof

3. Remove internal fixing screws (2)

4. Remove covers (3) from plates fixing windscreen (4)

5. Remove plate fixing screws (5) and winscreem

#### Installation - Page 9-13 to 9-15

1. Reverse removal procedure, noting:
   - Apply recommended adhesive to windscreen plates

**Windscreen Installation Notes - Page 9-15:**

**CAUTION:** This procedure describes the glass substitution using a component of urethane adhesive combined with a primer. Each adhesive has its own drying time and setting time, and should be used in accordance to each specific procedure. Neglecting the stated prescriptions for the correct use of the adhesive, could modify the adhesive strength. Consequently, before carrying out the work, you should read with care the instructions given by the manufacturer of the adhesive to be used, and keep strictly to each one of the stated cautions.

**Detailed Installation Steps:**
1. Using a clearing solvent, clean all around the windscreen where the glass is going to fix and let it dry for more than ten minutes

2. To determine the installing position of the glass in the body, put the glass in such a way that the distance between the glass borders and the body be uniform

3. Place aligning marks in glass and body

4. Clean properly the contact surfaces of the old adhesive, paint or naked metal

**NOTE:** If metal naked surfaces appear then apply body primer taking care of not applying it in the adhesive surfaces remaining in the body.

5. Clean the glass surfaces to be glued in the body with a clean rag and if you use cleaning solvent let it dry for more than ten minutes

6. Install new moulding in glass

7. Apply enough primer in the entire glass surface to be glued to the body

8. Apply primer for moulding in the moulding coupling face to the body

9. Apply recommended adhesive all the glass coupling face to windscreen

**NOTE:**
- Begin applying the adhesive from the lower side of glass
- Be careful full not to damage the primer
- The width of the adhesive strip must be 10 + 12 mm
- Press glass quickly against the body after applying adhesive
- Use a rubber suction pad to fix and transport the glass after applying the adhesive

10. Fix using rubber suction pad and place the glass against the body, aligning the marks you did and press

11. Inspect for impermeability, throwing water with a hose pipe over the windscreen

**NOTE:** Do not use highly pressured water either pressurized air nor infrared radiation for drying.

12. If there is water coming in, dry the glass and fill the entrance point with adhesive. If water keeps coming in dismantle the glass and repeat assembly.

13. Wipe the water used in the impermeability test and assemble the moulding.

## Seat Belts - Page 9-24 to 9-25

### Seat Belts Servicing - Page 9-24

Before doing the service or replacing seat belts, note the following remarks:

- Belts must be in normal position with respect to the retractor and/or buckle
- Keep away belts from sharp objects or others damaging them
- Pay attention no to bend neither damage any buckle parts and the belt tension
- Avoid whitening or dye the belt. (Use only soft soap and tepid water for cleaning)
- When installing the seat belt anchoring bolt, do it first by hand to avoid damaging the thread
- Don't tray to repair the retracting mechanism or its cover. Replace faulty pieces by new ones
- Keep always belts dry and clean
- If in doubt about the worthiness of any piece, replace it
- Replace seat belts with sliced or damaged bands
- Do not block the slit on panel through which the band passes

### Removal - Page 9-25

1. Remove screws fixing safety belt ensemble to body
2. Remove safety belt ensemble

#### Installation
1. Assemble safety belt unit reversing the dismantling order

### Inspection - Page 9-25

Seat belts and fixing pieces might affect to important components and systems of the vehicle. Consequently, they must be carefully inspected and substituted only by original pieces.

Inspect the followings components:

- **Seat belt:** The strip or belt must be free
- **Retractor:** Should block the strip when pulling it fast. Beside, the retractor should block strip when strip is inclined (approx 15°) in a longitudinal or transversal direction
- **Anchoring bolt:** Anchoring bolts must be tightened to the specified values
- **Belt hook:** Must stay blocked adequately

## Anticorrosion Treatment - Page 9-27 to 9-29

Steel sheets have been previously treated for corrosion. To get a higher resistance to corrosion inside and/or outside these are galvanized both sides.

Because of greater protection against corrosion some procedures are applied such as those described below:

1. Steel sheets have an electro cathodic priming to provide greater resistance to corrosion
2. A wax layer resistant to corrosion is applied to the internal doors and the main lateral frames, where there is a tendency to accumulate dampness
3. A vinyl coating is applied in the lower part of the body and inside of the wheel box
4. A sealer is applied in the border of the door, connection of plates in the engine compartment, and similar places, to avoid water entrance producing corrosion

**When replacing steel plates or repair after collision:**

When steel plates are replaced, or when a repair is done after a collision, if part of the repair is not treated, will be unprotected against corrosion and getting damaged. Hence, it is essential to do such protection when repairing the corresponding surfaces. All the metal sheets are covered with metal conditioner and primer when they come out from the factory. When repairs are done or pieces are replaced all the naked metal surfaces must be cleaned and covered with rust proof primer. Do this work before applying the sealer and the anticorrosion wax coating. Sealants are applied to specific unions of a vehicle during manufacturing. The sealant blocks dust entrance in the vehicle and acts as an anticorrosion barrier. Sealant is applied to the door, the border of the bonnet and between panelings. If any damages are repaired proper correction and fitting must be done. Seal again the connection joints at a new replaced sheet and the door border part or replaced bonnet. Use quality sealants to seal the earflap unions, overlaying unions and weldings. The sealant should be flexible and take on the paint after putting it on the repaired areas.

To seal open unions, use an impermeable material. Select sealant appropriate to such repair. Read the instruction given by the manufacturer before using the sealant.

At times repair must be painted, in these cases follow the normal painting and finishing procedures.

**Sequence of Work with Anticorrosive Materials - Page 9-28:**
1. Clean and prepare the metal surfaces
2. Apply primer
3. Apply sealer (all unions previously sealed)
4. Apply the adequate colour paint at all the part previously coloured
5. Apply anticorrosion compound (penetrating wax)
6. Apply protecting layer (rust proof material)

### Metallic Spare Pieces Finishing - Page 9-29

Metallic spare pieces (or ensembles) are covered with electrodeposited primer. To get good paint adhesion, the following procedure is recommended:

1. Use wax or solvent degreaser to clean the piece
2. Use damp or dry sandpaper (n° 400) to polish slightly the sheet. Do not polish strongly to avoid scratches
3. If the priming coating applied in the factory has been remove exposing the metal, apply a metal conditioner for protection. Read the manufacturer advices about how to use the metal conditioner
4. Apply the primer on dry surfaces before starting polishing with sandpaper. Drying time is specified in the primer container
5. Use damp or dry sandpaper (n° 400) to polish gently the sheet
6. Wash again this part
7. Apply adequate colour paint on top
8. Paints require different drying methods. Follow manufacturer instructions indicated in the paint container
9. When applying lacquer (fast drying paint layer), dry the painted surface and polish with a polishing compound. If melamine or acrylic is being used, no latter polishing after drying is required
10. If a lacquer coating is used, do no apply wax on the covered surface till full complete drying (about two months)

## Frame - Page 9-30 to 9-32

Each individual component of the framework has not only an effect on the vehicle stiffness and but also on wheels alignment (convergence, camber angle, castor angle).

Consequently, it is essential to carry out thorough surveys of the framework, doing if necessary weldings related to its correction or substitution.

When it is found that the plugging treatment or the anticorrosion one on the framework are damaged, it is compulsory to repair immediately followed by an anticorrosion treatment (see 9-27).
