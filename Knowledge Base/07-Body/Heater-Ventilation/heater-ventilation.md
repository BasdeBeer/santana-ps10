# Heater and Ventilation System (Section 1A)

**Source:** Santana PS10 Service Manual (791044-C1I), Pages 34-51

## System Overview

The heater and ventilation system is designed to provide permanent and pleasant ventilation through air outlets on the front panel. Hot air outlet is on the glove compartment side and air outlets for windscreen demisting are located on the upper sides of the glove compartment.

---

## General System Description

### System Components

The system consists of:

1. **Front side air outlet** - Provides demisting for windscreen
2. **Front center air outlet** - Central ventilation
3. **Side demister outlet** - Side window demisting
4. **Upper demister outlet** - Upper windscreen demisting
5. **Heater assembly** - Hot water heating core
6. **Demister duct** - Routes demisting air
7. **Air flow ducts** - Distributes heated/cooled air
8. **Heating unit** - Contains heater core and fan motor
9. **Protector** - Directs air flow
10. **Heating control panel** - Manual controls for heating
11. **Heating control levers unit** - Operating levers for heating
12. **Heater valve control cable** - Controls hot water flow
13. **Heater valve** - Regulates coolant flow to heater
14. **Heater control cable** - Controls air temperature
15. **Grommet** - Sealing component

---

## Heater Control Operation

The heating system control is based on the use and proper handling of four control levers:

### Control Lever A - Air Distribution

**Three working positions:**
1. **Ventilation position (1)** - Fresh air only, no heating
2. **Ventilation and demisting position (2)** - Combination mode
3. **Demisting position (3)** - Full demisting mode

### Control Lever B - Air Circulation

**Two working positions:**
1. **Air circulation position (4)** - Recirculates cabin air
2. **Fresh air position (5)** - Draws outside air

### Control Lever C - Temperature Control

Temperature control lever is temperature control lever. Air temperature can be controlled moving the lever to the chosen temperature.

**Positions:**
- Displaced totally to the left: Maximum hot air
- Displaced totally to the right: Maximum fresh air

### Control Lever D - Fan Speed Control

Control lever D is air speed selection lever. Fan speed increases when lever is displaced from left to right.

**Positions:** 0, 1, 2, 3 (OFF to HIGH)

---

## Heating System Operation Modes

### Mode 1: Forced Ventilation

**Configuration:** Control levers positioned for outside air intake, demister outlet, and fan operation.

**Air Flow Path:**
- Outside air enters through front grille
- Air flows through demister ducts
- Exits through windscreen demisting vents

### Mode 2: Heating with Outside Air Introduction

**Configuration:** Heater valve open, heating core active, fresh outside air drawn in.

**Air Flow Path:**
- Outside air enters and passes through heater core
- Hot water from engine coolant heats the air
- Air distributed through heating outlets

### Mode 3: Heating with Inside Air Circulation

**Configuration:** Heater activated, inside air recirculated through heating unit.

**Air Flow Path:**
- Inside cabin air drawn back to heater
- Passes through heating core
- Distributed back to cabin at controlled temperature

## On-Vehicle Service

### Ventilator and Diffusor Unit

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Disconnect negative cable at battery
2. Disconnect connector at ventilator and diffusor unit
3. Remove air recirculation lever cable bolt (1) front ventilator and diffusor unit
4. Remove ventilator nuts (2)
5. Extract unit (3)

**Installation:**
1. Reverse removal procedures

---

### Heater Valve

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Remove bolts (1) from tank
2. Remove degassing tank without forcing water hoses
3. Disconnect opening and closing drive from valve (2)
4. Disconnect water inlet and outlet hoses (3) and (4) from valve and cover up them
5. Remove fixing nuts and valve

**Installation:**

1. Reverse removal procedures
2. Refill cooling system with proper coolant (refer to Section 0B-8)
3. Check each joint for leakage with motor running

---

### Heater Blower Motor Switch

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Remove heating system control levers (see 1A-9)
2. Remove heater blower motor switch nuts
3. Remove switch, disconnect connector and check continuity from terminal to terminal

**Installation:**

1. Install new switch if necessary
2. Reverse removal procedure, noting tightening torques

---

### Control Cable Adjustment

To adjust any control cable:
1. Put the control lever at the OFF position
2. Place the corresponding acting element at the OFF position and fix the cable

---

### Heating Control Cables and Levers

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Remove control levers hub caps (1) from front levers and withdraw control panel (2)
2. Remove fixing screws (3) on glove box where levers unit (4) is located
3. Remove glove box (see 1A-10)
4. Remove control cables from their unions connecting to:
   - Ventilator
   - Protector
   - Heater
   - Valve
5. Remove heating control levers unit (4)

**Installation:**

1. Install new lever unit set if required
2. Install other components reversing removal procedures
3. Note tightening torques to specified values
4. Proceed to adjust control cables (see 1A-8)

---

### Heating Unit - Removal and Installation

**Complete Glove Box Removal Required**

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Remove steering wheel (see 3C-3)
2. Remove combined drives (see 3C-5)
3. Remove ventilator nozzles (1) and clock (2)
4. Remove hub caps (3), control panel (4) and levers unit screws
5. Remove glove box nuts and fixing clips
6. Disconnect glove box wiring connections
7. Remove glove box

**Heater Removal:**

8. Disconnect drive cable from front heater unit (7)
9. Drain radiator; disconnect water hoses from heater unit and cover up them
10. Remove fixing nuts (8) and bolt (9) from heater (7)
11. Remove heater unit

**Installation:**

1. Install heater unit reversing removal procedure, noting tightening torques
2. Add refrigerant till reaching correct level (see 0B-8 under MAINTENANCE AND LUBRICATION)
3. Check there are no leaks with motor on

---

## Inside Ventilation

When air admission control lever is at fresh air position, outside air enters inside the vehicle through the admission grille to be found in the front part of the windscreen, and comes through the ventilation outlet located in the back side of the vehicle roof.

## Key Specifications

### Fluid Capacity
- **Total Circuit Capacity:** 12 litres (see Maintenance Section 0B)
- **Coolant Mixture:** 50% demineralized water + 50% Dinagel 9103

### Temperature Range
- **Operating Temperature:** 94°C to 110°C
- **Freezing Protection:** -38°C

### Important Notes

- Use only recommended heating liquids
- Do not open expansion vase while engine and radiator are hot
- Clean filter regularly for optimal performance
- Check control cable operation frequently
- Maintain proper coolant level

---

## Safety Warnings

**WARNING:** Do not open expansion vase while engine and radiator are hot; high temperature liquid and vapours could be spouted and bring about serious bodily harm.

**CAUTION:** The coolant mixture should be revised in summer and in winter, as it works as an anti-freezer, refrigerant, corrosion protector and lubricating agent.
