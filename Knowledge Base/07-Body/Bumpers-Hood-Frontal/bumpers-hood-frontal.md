# Section 2: Bumpers, Front Hood and Frontal

## Overview

This section covers the service procedures for the bumper systems, front hood assembly, and frontal components of the Santana PS10 vehicle. All fasteners used in these assemblies are critical safety components that affect vehicle performance and system integrity.

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** Fasteners are important attaching parts that could affect performance of vital components and systems, and may entail significant repair expenses. Replacement pieces must use the same replacement reference number or equivalent. Do not use substitutes of inferior quality. When assembling, ensure proper retention of all parts.

## Front Bumper

### Components
The front bumper assembly consists of:
1. **Front Bumper** - Main structural component
2. **Bumper Grille** - Upper protective mesh
3. **Side Thermo Conformed Pad** - Lateral impact absorption pads
4. **Center Thermo Conformed Pad** - Central impact absorption pad

### Service Notes
- The front bumper is a critical safety component
- Thermo-conformed pads provide energy absorption in frontal impacts
- All fasteners must be properly tightened to specification

---

## Rear Bumper

### Components
The rear bumper assembly consists of:
1. **Rear Bumper** - Main structural component
2. **Side Pad** - Lateral impact absorption pads
3. **Center Pad** - Central impact absorption pad

### Service Notes
- The rear bumper protects the vehicle from rear-end collisions
- Similar pad configuration to front bumper for impact protection
- All mounting points must be properly secured

## Front Hood

### Components
The front hood assembly includes:
1. **Hood** - Main structural panel (aluminum or steel)
2. **Hinges** - Left and right hood hinges
3. **Lock Cable** - Hood release mechanism cable
4. **Lock Assembly** - Hood latch assembly
5. **Cushion** - Hood-to-body contact cushions
6. **Stay** - Hood support stay for vertical positioning

### Design Features
- The hood provides access to the engine compartment
- Hinges allow for full opening angle
- Latch system ensures secure closure during operation
- Support stay holds hood in open position safely

---

## Removal and Installation of Front Hood

### Removal Procedure

1. Remove the four fixing hood screws (1) located at the hinge attachment points
2. Withdraw the hood carefully from the vehicle

### Installation Procedure

1. Install hood reversing the dismantling steps
2. Carry out hood adjustment steps as required for correct assembly (see page 2-6)

### Important Notes
- Support the hood with adequate assistance to prevent dropping
- Ensure all four screws are removed before withdrawing hood
- Hood must be properly aligned before final tightening

---

## Front Hood Adjustments

### Longitudinal and Transversal Adjustment

**Procedure:**
1. Loosen the four screws (1) at the hinge attachment
2. Adjust hood lengthwise (forward/backward) as needed
3. Adjust hood transversally (left/right) as needed
4. Once properly positioned, retighten the screws to secure

**Purpose:** Ensures proper alignment of hood with body line and ensures consistent gap around hood perimeter.

### Vertical Adjustment

**Condition Requiring Adjustment:**
- If one hood side is not level with the adjacent fender

**Adjustment Method:**
1. Adjust hood height by loosening or tightening hood cushion (2)
2. Height cushions control vertical hood-to-body positioning
3. Adjust until hood is flush with fender line on both sides

**Torque Specification for Hood Cushion:**
- Tighten to manufacturer specification as required

### Hood Lock Adjustment

**Procedure:**
1. Loosen lock hood assembly screws (3) of the bonnet closing unit
2. Adjust anchoring to position latch correctly
3. Ensure latch engages smoothly when hood is closed
4. Tighten screws (3) once positioned correctly

**Purpose:** Ensures hood closes securely and latches properly without forcing or binding.

## Frontal Components

### Assembly Components

The frontal assembly (front panel area) includes:
1. **Front Grille** - Upper front intake grille
2. **Fender** - Left and right front fenders
3. **Register Cover** - Cooling register covers
4. **Front Frame** - Main structural front frame member

### Functions
- Provides vehicle front structural support
- Protects radiator and cooling system
- Houses lighting components
- Provides mounting points for bumper system

## Fender

### Removal Procedure

1. Remove the front bumpers and grille (reference front bumper section)
2. Disconnect lateral turn signal lamp
3. Remove head lights from fender pulling till removal
4. Remove head lights regulator
5. Remove components found at the interior of the fender accordingly:
   - Supports
   - Tanks
   - Other internal components
6. Remove other fender fixing elements and withdraw them
7. Remove fender fixing screws to dashboard and front frame
8. Withdraw the fender

### Removal Sequence Notes
- Work systematically from top to bottom
- Disconnect all electrical connectors before removal
- Support components during removal to prevent damage
- Document component locations before removal

### Installation Procedure

1. Install fender and all its components, reversing dismantling steps
2. Ensure all components are properly repositioned in interior
3. Reconnect all electrical connectors
4. Install fender fixing screws to dashboard and frame

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** Apply a final tightening once all fixing screws have been fixed in place to ensure even clamping pressure and proper body alignment.

## Technical Specifications Summary

### Hood Specifications
- **Type:** Hinged access panel
- **Material:** Aluminum or steel construction
- **Attachment:** Four-point screw fastening
- **Adjustment Range:** Full length/width/height adjustment capability

### Front Bumper Specifications
- **Impact Pads:** Thermo-conformed material for energy absorption
- **Mounting:** Multi-point fastening system
- **Material:** Steel or composite main body with foam padding

### Rear Bumper Specifications
- **Impact Pads:** Side and center thermo-conformed pads
- **Mounting:** Multi-point fastening system
- **Material:** Similar construction to front bumper

### Fender Specifications
- **Type:** Bolt-on body panel
- **Fastening Points:** Dashboard and front frame
- **Components:** Integral mounting for lights, supports, and hardware

## Service Tools and Special Equipment

- Standard screwdriver set
- Metric wrench set (for fasteners)
- Socket set for hood screws
- Protective covering for body during work
- Hood support prop or assistant

---

## Safety Cautions

- Always support hood properly during removal to prevent pinching hands or dropping on vehicle
- Disconnect battery negative terminal before working with electrical components (lights, latches)
- Ensure work area is clear of obstructions
- Have adequate lighting for interior component work
- Use proper lifting techniques for fender removal
- Wear safety glasses when working overhead
