# Santana PS10 Engine General Information

**Engine:** Iveco 8140.43P, 2.8 Turbo Diesel (Euro III)
**Source:** Service Manual PS10 - Section 6-2
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-26

---

## Engine Specifications

### Basic Engine Data

**Engine Type:** 4-cylinder, in-line, turbocharged diesel with intercooler

**Physical Dimensions:**
- **Bore:** 94.4 mm
- **Stroke:** 100 mm
- **Total Displacement:** 2,800 cc (2.8 liters)
- **Number of Cylinders:** 4
- **Valve Configuration:** In-line

**Compression and Performance:**
- **Compression Ratio:** 18.5:1
- **Engine Code (Mechanical Injection):** 8140.43C.3
- **Engine Code (Common Rail):** 8140.43S.4

### Engine Block Specifications

**Material:** Cast iron
**Bore Tolerance:** Specified in detailed technical drawings
**Main Bearing Journals:** Four main bearings supporting the crankshaft

## Crankshaft Specifications and Tolerances

### Crankshaft Journal Measurements

The crankshaft includes the following critical journals that must be maintained within precise tolerances:

**Main Bearing Journals:**
- Number of journals: 4
- Proper rectification required for journal wear
- Tolerance control critical for engine performance

**Connecting Rod Journals:**
- Number of journals: 4
- Size and tolerance specifications must be verified
- Run-out limits and straightness critical

### Crankshaft Rectification Procedures

When mechanical rectification is required:

1. Journal surfaces must be rectified perpendicular to crankshaft axis
2. Compression force specifications apply during rectification
3. Multiple passes may be required for surface finish
4. Final journal diameter tolerance must be verified with precision calipers
5. Journals must be concentric to the crankshaft centerline

## Piston and Connecting Rod Assembly

### Piston Specifications

**Piston Design:** T-slot design for improved oil cooling

**Ring Groove Clearances:**
- **Compression Ring 1:** 0.05 to 0.11 mm
- **Compression Ring 2:** 0.03 to 0.09 mm
- **Oil Control Ring:** 0.15 to 0.35 mm

**Piston Pin (Gudgeon Pin):**
- **Type:** Fully floating
- **Diameter:** Specified on individual component
- **Length:** Critical for connecting rod fit
- **Material:** High-strength steel

### Connecting Rod

**Type:** I-beam design
**Material:** Forged steel
**Bearing Type:** Bushed at gudgeon pin end, precision insert bearing at crankshaft end

**Connecting Rod Bushings:**
- **Type:** Phosphor bronze or sintered iron
- **Interference fit required**
- **Reaming required after installation**
- **Clearance with pin:** 0.02-0.04 mm

### Clearance Specifications

**Piston-to-Bore Clearance:**
- Varies by bore position
- Must be measured at specified locations
- Cold (20°C) and hot (80°C) clearances differ

**Connecting Rod Side Clearance:**
- Measured at the crankshaft journal
- Proper clearance prevents rod binding
- Excessive clearance indicates wear

**Connecting Rod Length:**
- Factory specification: Verified on assembly
- Rod length affects piston position and compression
- Replacement rods must match original specification

## Valve Specifications

### Valve Dimensions

**Intake Valves:**
- **Stem Diameter:** 7 mm
- **Head Diameter:** Approximately 37 mm
- **Length:** To be verified from detailed drawings

**Exhaust Valves:**
- **Stem Diameter:** 7 mm
- **Head Diameter:** Approximately 31 mm
- **Length:** To be verified from detailed drawings

### Valve Guide Specifications

**Type:** Cast iron or bronze inserts
**Installation:** Press fit into cylinder head
**Inside Diameter:** 7.00 to 7.025 mm

**Valve Stem Clearance:**
- Intake: 0.02-0.05 mm
- Exhaust: 0.04-0.07 mm

**Valve Guide Substitution:**
- Remove old guides with press or puller tool
- New guides must be pressed to correct depth
- Ream inside diameter after installation
- Proper seating essential for leak-free operation

### Valve Seat Specifications

**Valve Seat Angles:**
- **Intake:** 45 degrees
- **Exhaust:** 45 degrees

**Valve Seat Inserts:**
- Hardened steel composition
- Press-fit into cylinder head
- Can be rebored if damaged

**Valve Seat Reboring:**
- Special carbide cutters required
- Concentric to valve guide
- Face angle: 45 degrees for both intake and exhaust
- Width specification: 1.5-2.0 mm

### Valve Spring Specifications

**Number of Springs:** Two per valve (dual spring design)
**Spring Type:** Coil springs with retainers and keepers

**Spring Pressure Specifications:**
- **Closed Height:** Measured at specific length
- **Open Height:** Measured at maximum valve lift
- Free length and installed load critical for proper operation

**Valve Seat and Face Angle:**
- **Seat Angle:** 45 degrees
- **Face Angle:** 45 degrees
- Proper angle ensures efficient sealing

## Timing System Specifications

### Timing Belt

**Type:** Toothed belt drive
**Material:** Reinforced rubber with teeth
**Number of Teeth:** Specified for synchronization accuracy

**Timing Marks:**
- Camshaft gear alignment mark (A)
- Crankshaft/flywheel alignment mark (B)
- Plunger cover reference mark (C)

**Belt Tension Specification (HSN High Strength Nitrile belt):**
- Measured at point (D) with frequency meter
- **Tension Range:** 88 to 112 Hz
- Tool reference: 00000A01484

**Timing Belt Replacement Interval:**
- Every 30,000 km (or more frequently if oil leaks detected)
- Replace if front crankshaft oil seal leaks
- Critical for engine timing accuracy

### Timing Belt Tensioner

**Tensioner Roller:** Spring-loaded, adjustable

**Tensioning Procedure:**
1. Apply 2.8 ± 3.0 Kg/m torque to crankshaft to pre-tension belt
2. Install tensioner nut and washers
3. Apply specified tightening torque (see Torque Specifications section)
4. Verify final tension at point (D)

## Cylinder Head Specifications

### Cylinder Head Assembly

**Type:** 4-valve per cylinder (2 intake, 2 exhaust)
**Material:** Aluminum alloy
**Gasket Type:** Multi-layer steel gasket (MLS)

**Cylinder Head Fasteners:**
- **Bolt Type:** High-strength steel
- **Tightening Sequence:** Specified in repair procedures
- **Torque Value:** See Torque Specifications section

### Camshaft Support Specifications

The cylinder head contains precision machined surfaces for camshaft support.

**Camshaft Support Diameter:**
- Critical measurement for bearing clearance
- Must be measured before installation
- Wear tolerance limits specified

**Camshaft Run-out Limits:**
- Total run-out: Not to exceed specification
- Measured on precision V-blocks
- Worn camshafts require replacement

### Valve Play (Tappet Clearance)

**Specifications:**
- **Inlet:** 0.5 ± 0.05 mm
- **Outlet:** 0.5 ± 0.05 mm

**Measurement Procedure:**
1. Engine cold or hot (specification applies to both conditions)
2. Rotate crankshaft until valve to be measured is closed
3. Measure clearance between rocker arm and valve stem
4. Record each valve measurement for reference

**Adjustment:**
- Adjusting shims (plates) available in various thicknesses
- Select appropriate shim thickness based on measured clearance
- Install shim with thickness engraved face toward rocker arm
- Re-measure after adjustment
