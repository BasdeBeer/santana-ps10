# Santana PS10 EIII - Lubrication System Training Manual

**Engine:** Iveco 8140.43P (2.8 TD, Euro III)
**Source:** Dealer Training Manual - Lubrication System (Pages 2-5)

---

## General Description

The engine lubrication is of type forced circulation and is brought about by the following elements:

### Lubrication System Components

- **Gears Oil Pump** - Incorporated in the auxiliary organs group and a pressure regulating valve incorporated to the auxiliary organs group rear cover

- **Modine Type Heat Exchanger** - With incorporated safety valve

- **Oil Filter (Double Filtrated)** - With incorporated safety valve

### System Operation Overview

Oil is sucked by the pump through a sucker plunged in the oil pan. Oil comes under pressure to the heat exchanger where it cools, goes through the oil filter, arriving later to the different organs in motion of engine to be lubricated through conduits and pipes.

When the lubrication cycle is accomplished oil returns to the oil pan by gravity.

**Safety Feature:** In case the oil filter is blocked or the heat exchanger is blocked, any of both would be excluded of lubrication circuit through its safety valve avoiding thus that the engine becomes without lubrication.

## Oil Vapours Recycling Circuit

The engine ventilation and oil vapours recycling circuit is constituted by the following components:

### Oil Vapours Recycling Components

- **Ramp** - Located in the upper part of oil pan of the auxiliary organs group. Oil vapours from the oil pan are collected while running and are sent to the oil condenser. At the same time, the ramp fulfils the function of spout

- **Oil Condenser** - Receiving the vapours coming from the ramp condensing them and sending them again to the oil pan. At the same time, the eventual exceeding vapours which can not be condensed are vented to the atmosphere through a pipe located in the upper part

### Oil Vapours Recycling Sketch

**Components (numbered):**

1. **Ramp for Collecting Oil Vapours and Oil Spout** - Upper oil pan collection
2. **Pipe Sending Oil Vapours to Condenser** - Vapor line
3. **Oil Vapours Condenser** - Condensing unit
4. **Condenser Oil Pipe Conduit to Oil Pan** - Return line
5. **Excess Oil Vapours Pipe Conduit for Venting to the Atmosphere** - Atmospheric vent
6. **Oil Pan** - Reservoir
7. **Auxiliary Organs Group** - Pump and accessory location

## Oil System Pressures and Operation

### Oil Pressure Ranges

**At Idle (Ticking Over Regime):**
- Minimum: 0.8 bar
- Specification: > 0.8 bar

**At Maximum RPM:**
- Minimum: 3.5 bar
- Specification: > 3.5 bar

### Pressure Regulation

The pressure regulating valve is incorporated to the auxiliary organs group rear cover and maintains the pressure within the system parameters.

### Heat Exchanger Function

- **Type**: Modine type with incorporated safety valve
- **Purpose**: Cools the high-temperature oil from the pump
- **Safety Feature**: Integral bypass valve prevents starvation if blocked

### Oil Filter System

- **Type**: Double filtrated
- **Integrated Components**: Safety valve
- **Function**: Removes contaminants while maintaining pressure supply
- **Safety Feature**: Bypass valve opens if filter becomes blocked

## Oil Flow Diagram - Schematic

**Primary Oil Distribution:**

1. Oil suction from pan through pump strainer
2. High-pressure pump delivery to heat exchanger
3. Heat exchanger cooling and pressure regulation
4. Distribution through main oil filter
5. Parallel circuits:
   - Main bearing lubrication
   - Camshaft drive lubrication
   - Auxiliary organs group lubrication
   - Turbocharger supply (if equipped)
   - Piston crown cooling injectors
6. Return to oil pan by gravity

## Lubrication Maintenance

### Oil Change Intervals

**Normal Conditions:**
- Every 15,000 km (9,000 miles) or 12 months

**Severe Driving Conditions:**
- Every 15,000 km (9,000 miles) or 12 months (same as normal)

### Oil Filter Replacement

**Normal Conditions:**
- Every 15,000 km (9,000 miles) or 12 months

**Important Notes:**
- Always replace oil filter when changing oil
- Use OEM specification filters only
- Ensure proper seating of new filter
- Fill filter with new oil before installation to reduce startup wear

### Oil Level Checking

- Check only on cold engine
- Use dipstick provided
- Maintain level between MIN and MAX marks
- Check every 1,000 km or weekly

### Fluid Type Compliance

- **Required**: SAE 10W/40, API SJ/CF diesel engine oil
- **Alternative**: Equivalent quality oils meeting above specifications
- **Warning**: Never mix different oil brands or types
- **Disposal**: Used oil must be disposed of properly per environmental regulations

## Safety Valves and Pressure Relief

### Heat Exchanger Safety Valve
- **Function**: Prevents overpresuization of heat exchanger
- **Opening Pressure**: Set by design (typically 3-5 bar above normal operating)
- **Bypass Path**: Routes pressure back to suction side

### Oil Filter Safety Valve
- **Function**: Prevents filter blockage from starving engine
- **Opening Pressure**: Calibrated to engage if filter clogs
- **Bypass Path**: Routes unfiltered oil to engine (less ideal but prevents damage)

**Important:** If safety valve opens, service oil and filter immediately

## Turbocharger Oil Supply (if equipped)

- **Supply Source**: Main lubrication circuit
- **Pressure**: Full system pressure maintained
- **Return**: Through dedicated drain line back to oil pan
- **Condition**: Clean oil critical for turbo bearing life

## Auxiliary Organs Group Lubrication

The auxiliary organs group includes:
- Gears oil pump
- Pressure regulating valve
- Related bearings and gears

**Lubrication Method**: Internal circuit from main pump discharge
**Cooling**: Integrated with main heat exchanger function

## Troubleshooting Oil System Problems

| Problem | Possible Cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Low Oil Pressure | Worn pump, clogged filter, low oil level | Check level, change filter, test pump |
| High Oil Pressure | Stuck pressure valve, wrong oil viscosity | Check valve operation, verify oil type |
| Oil Leaks | Worn seals, loose plugs, damaged gaskets | Identify source, replace seals/gaskets |
| Dirty Oil (Dark/Black) | Normal operation or extended interval | Change oil and filter |
| Oil Foam/Bubbles | Overfilled, coolant contamination, aeration | Drain excess, check for leaks, inspect |
| Oil Temperature High | Excessive load, stuck thermostat, low flow | Check operation, verify cooling system |
| Burning Oil Smell | Leaks burning on engine, worn rings | Inspect for leaks, check compression |

## System Maintenance Checklist

**Before Each Start:**
- [ ] Check oil level (cold engine)
- [ ] Visual inspection for leaks

**Every Oil Change (15,000 km / 12 months):**
- [ ] Change engine oil to specification
- [ ] Replace oil filter
- [ ] Inspect drain plug and pan for damage
- [ ] Check for leaks after starting

**Every 30,000 km:**
- [ ] Inspect oil hoses for condition
- [ ] Check heat exchanger for blockage (visual)
- [ ] Verify oil pressure readings

**Every 60,000 km:**
- [ ] Deep inspection of lubrication system
- [ ] Flush and refill if contaminated
- [ ] Service turbocharger oil return (if equipped)

## Safety Precautions

1. **Never check oil on a hot engine** - Wait 5-10 minutes after shutdown
2. **Always use correct oil type** - Wrong viscosity damages engine
3. **Do not overfill** - Excess oil causes pressure issues and foam
4. **Handle used oil safely** - It contains contaminants and toxins
5. **Replace filter every oil change** - Dirty filters compromise system
6. **Allow new oil to circulate** - Run engine gently for first few miles after oil change
7. **Never run engine without oil** - Catastrophic damage occurs in seconds
