# Santana PS10 Exhaust System

**Engine:** Iveco 8140.43P, 2.8 Turbo Diesel (Euro III)
**Source:** Service Manual PS10 - Section 6K
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-26

---

## Exhaust System Overview

The Santana PS10 diesel engine uses a turbocharged exhaust system with emission control to meet Euro II standards. The exhaust system consists of the exhaust manifold, turbocharger, intercooler, and emission control components.

---

## Turbocharger System

### Turbocharger Models

Two turbocharger variants are used on the Iveco 8140.43P:

**Primary Turbocharger:**
- **Model:** MITSUBISHI TF0 HM 13 T-6
- **Type:** Fixed geometry turbocharger with waste gate control
- **Application:** Standard configuration

**Alternative Turbocharger:**
- **Model:** MITSUBISHI TD04 (variant)
- **Application:** Alternative configuration

### Turbocharger Operation

The turbocharger uses exhaust gases to drive a turbine, compressing intake air. Key features:

1. **Air Compression:** Turbocharger pressurizes intake air before entry into the engine cylinders
2. **Pressure Limitation:** Mechanical pressure limitation varies turbine revolution speed, directing overfeed air directly to the exhaust pipe when calibrated value is reached
3. **Lubrication:** Turbocharger is lubricated using engine oil
4. **Cooling:** Turbocharger rotates at very high speeds and requires adequate cooling

### Turbocharger Checking

**Before Intervention:**
- Verify turbocharger operation without dismantling
- Check for abnormal noise or vibration
- Inspect for oil leaks at seals

**Visual Inspection Points:**
- Turbocharger bearing condition
- Seal integrity at oil inlet/outlet
- Compressor wheel for damage
- Turbine wheel for erosion or scoring

### Turbocharger Pressure Regulation

**Pressure Limiting Valve (Waste Gate):**
- Mechanically limits maximum turbo pressure
- Bypasses excess exhaust gases when maximum pressure reached
- Protects engine from over-boost conditions
- Requires periodic checking for proper operation

## Intercooler System

### Intercooler Function

The intercooler cools compressed air exiting the turbocharger before it enters the engine cylinders.

**Design Type:** Air-to-air intercooler with water radiator integration

**Components:**
- Air radiator (intercooler) mounted with water radiator
- Metal interconnection pipes
- Air channeling pieces
- Thermometric switch
- Air inlet and outlet manifolds

### Intercooler Specifications

**Intercooler Removal and Installation:**
- Intercooler removal requires disconnection of rigid interconnection pipe from air channeling part
- Four fixing screws must be removed
- Intercooler can be separated from water radiator for service
- Air hose connections remain intact during removal

**Intercooler Exterior Cleaning:**
- Use pressurized water and compressed air
- Brush gently to avoid damage to cooling fins
- No pointed implements to be used
- Professional cleaning recommended for efficiency restoration

### Intercooler Integration

The intercooler is integral to the cooling system. When replacing water radiator components:
- Air radiator/intercooler (11) is fixed with four screws (17)
- Metal interconnection pipe (16) connects to air channeling
- Hose assembly (18) requires disconnection during radiator removal
- Air channeling piece (20) is fixed with four screws (21)
- Thermometric switch (22) monitors coolant temperature

## Exhaust Manifold

The exhaust manifold collects exhaust gases from all four cylinders and directs them to the turbocharger turbine.

**Location:** Mounted directly on the cylinder head

**Gasket:** Metal composite gasket seals manifold to cylinder head

**Fasteners:** Multiple studs and nuts secure manifold to cylinder head

**Connection to Turbocharger:** Direct flange connection to turbocharger inlet

## Engine Emissions Specifications (Euro II)

The Iveco 8140.43P meets Euro II emission standards with the following limits:

### Exhaust Gas Emissions

**Standards Compliance:**
- Conforms to Euro2 normative on gas emissions (bench measure engine cycle R49)
- Limits fixed by normative CEE 91/542

**Emission Limits (for both variants):**

| Emission Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| CO (Carbon monoxide) | < 4.0 g/kWh |
| NO (Nitrogen monoxide) | < 7.0 g/kWh |
| HC (Non-combusted hydrocarbons) | < 1.1 g/kWh |

**Gas Emissions (Mechanical Injection 8140.43C):**
- Without catalyser: < 0.15 g/kWh
- Test fuel: CEC RFT 73-T-90-S ± 0.05%

**Gas Emissions (Common Rail 8140.43S):**
- With catalyser: < 0.15 g/kWh

### Exhaust Fumes Specifications

**Standards Compliance:**
- Conforms to normative on exhaust fumes limit
- Limits fixed by normative CEE 72/306 and R 24-03

**Opacity Measurements (Bosch BSU opacimeter degrees):**

**Mechanical Injection Engine (8140.43C):**
- Maximum power (3800 r.p.m.): 2.0 degrees
- Maximum torque: 2.5 degrees
- Full load at 1000 rpm: 3.5 degrees

**Common Rail Engine (8140.43S):**
- Maximum power (3800 r.p.m.): 1.5 degrees
- Maximum torque: 2.5 degrees
- Full load at 1000 rpm: 3.5 degrees

### Acoustic Emission Specifications

**Standards:** ISO std.3474 (microphones placed 1m from engine surfaces)

**Mechanical Injection Engine (8140.43C):**
- Minimum (800 r.p.m.): dB A 79
- Maximum power (3800 r.p.m.): dB A 99

**Common Rail Engine (8140.43S):**
- Minimum (800 r.p.m.): dB A 76
- Maximum power (3800 r.p.m.): dB A 96

## Air Intake System

The air intake system supplies filtered and cooled air to the turbocharger and engine.

### Air Intake Components

**Air Filter:**
- Type: Dry air filter
- Filter element: Replaceable cartridge
- Intake manifold: Distributes air from turbocharger

**Air Flow Path:**
1. Ambient air drawn through air filter (removing dust particles)
2. Air enters turbocharger compressor
3. Compressed air passes through intercooler (cooling to improve density)
4. Cold air outlet from intercooler directed to intake manifold
5. Intake manifold distributes air to cylinders
6. Pressure tube to LDA device (mechanical injection engines only)

### Pressure Tube to LDA (Mechanical Injection Engines)

**Function:** Displays turbocharger pressure to mechanical injection advance device

**Location:** Connected to intake manifold pressure point

**Usage:** LDA device uses this signal to vary fuel injection timing based on turbo boost pressure

## Service and Maintenance

### Exhaust System Safety Precautions

- Turbocharger shaft rotates at very high speeds (80,000+ RPM)
- Never touch or attempt to rotate turbo turbine wheel when engine is running
- Allow engine to cool before service work on exhaust components
- High temperature caution: Exhaust system reaches extreme temperatures during operation

### Checking Before Intervention

**Pre-service Inspection:**
1. Visually inspect for oil leaks at turbocharger seals
2. Listen for abnormal turbo noise (grinding, whistling, or loud spooling)
3. Check for exhaust system leaks
4. Verify intercooler for external damage or clogging
5. Inspect air intake system for blockage

### Cleaning and Inspection

**Turbocharger Inspection:**
- Check compressor wheel for blade damage
- Inspect turbine housing for erosion
- Verify bearing clearance without disassembly when possible

**Intercooler Service:**
- External cleaning with pressurized water and air
- Verify inlet/outlet connections are secure
- Check for internal blockage if cooling efficiency reduced

**Exhaust Manifold:**
- Inspect gasket condition
- Check for cracks in manifold
- Verify all fasteners are tight

### Wastegate Actuator Details

**Type:** Internal wastegate with mechanical actuator (diaphragm + spring)
**Connection:** Actuator arm attached to wastegate flap via screw thread with nut (adjustable)

**Operating principle:**
- At rest / low boost: spring holds actuator rod **retracted (in)** → wastegate flap **closed** → all exhaust drives turbine
- As boost rises: pressure acts on diaphragm → rod extends **out** → opens wastegate flap → exhaust bypasses turbine → limits boost
- Rod stuck out = wastegate permanently open = underboost + exhaust whistle (typically audible at 2000-2500 RPM / ~100 km/h)

**Replacement actuator:** Search for "49377-07000 wastegate actuator" or "49377-07010 actuator" — actuator-only replacements are available (€30-85). Ensure replacement has same boost pressure rating (bar) as original.

### Wastegate Maintenance

- **Lubrication:** Use MoS2 (molybdenum disulfide) penetrating oil on the wastegate pivot and actuator rod. Do NOT use silicone oil — it burns off at exhaust temperatures.
- **Inspection:** Periodically check that actuator rod moves freely with spring resistance. Rod should retract when released.
- **Common failure:** Actuator rod or pivot seizes due to carbon/soot buildup and heat cycling. Symptoms: loss of boost, exhaust whistle at mid-RPM.

---

### Wastegate Issue Log (24-BX-HS)

**Date:** April 2026 (~121,400 km)
**Symptom:** Whistling sound at ~100 km/h / 2000-2500 RPM
**Diagnosis:** Wastegate actuator rod stuck in extended (out) position, holding wastegate flap permanently open. Wastegate valve flap moves freely when actuator disconnected — problem is inside the actuator (seized rod or failed diaphragm/spring).
**Temporary fix:** MoS2 penetrating oil applied to actuator rod and pivot, worked rod back and forth to free it.
**Permanent fix:** Replace wastegate actuator (49377-07000 / 49377-07010 compatible).
