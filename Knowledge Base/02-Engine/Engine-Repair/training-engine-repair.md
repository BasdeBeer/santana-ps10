## 2. Timing Belt Substitution

### 2.1 Removal Procedure

1. Turn crankshaft in the rotating direction until aligning mark [A] of camshaft gear with mark [B] of plungers cover
2. Fit tool [C] through hole [22] from flywheel housing [23], in such a way that is lodged in the engine flywheel milling [21]
3. In the same position, fit tool in hole [25] of organs group driving gear [24], in such a way that it fit in the hole of block located in front of gear hole [25]
4. Remove nut [33] fixing the tensioning roller [32]
5. Remove lower cover [26] of timing

**Critical Alignment Points:**
- Three control points must be synchronized
- If not properly aligned, the belt is too tight or badly placed
- Belt replacement interval: Every 30,000 km (more often if detected)
- Replace timing belt if oil leaks from front crankshaft oil-seal detected

### 2.2 Installation Procedure

1. Install new timing belt [28], ensuring correct coupling in teeth of gears and correct tensioning of both stretches
2. Withdraw key [31] to allow beam [30] acts over the tensor roller [32] and put belt under tension
3. Withdraw tools [C] to synchronies gear [24] and flywheel [21]
4. Turn crankshaft two complete turns till aligning marks [A] and [B]
5. Install timing lower cover [26]; tighten screws to the specified tightening torque
6. Install nut and washers fixing tensioning roller [32] and apply the specified tightening torque: **4 Kg-m (40 Nm)**

### 2.3 Timing Belt Tension Checking

1. Apply torque of 2.8 ± 3.0 Kg/m to crankshaft [34] in the rotating direction, to put belt slightly tensioned
2. Install nut and washers fixing tensioning roller [32] and apply the specified tightening torque: **4 Kg-m (40 Nm)**
3. Check that belt tension at point [D] agrees with the specified value: **88 ± 112 Hz**

## 3. Valves Play

### 3.1 Checking the Valves Play

1. Use the appropriate tool on the crankshaft pulley screw to be able to turn it
2. Turn camshaft till achieving the closure of valves to be checked
3. Measure with calliper depths part [B] the play between plungers and inlet and outlet connecting rods

**Valves Play Specification:**
- Inlet/Outlet: **0.5 ± 0.05 mm**

**Important Notes:**
- The value given for the valves play is with the engine hot or cold
- Write down values read in each valve to carry out later the adequate adjustments if necessary

## 5. Cylinders Block

### 5.1 Measurement of Cylinders Block

**Measurement Procedure:**
1. Control the level of wear, oval and conical shaping of cylinders use a calliper for interiors [1] provided with centesimal dial gauge [2] previously set to zero with ring calliper [3]
2. Carry out three measurements at three different heights of the cylinder as shown and at two planes perpendicular one to another. One plane is parallel to the longitudinal axle of engine [A] and another perpendicular to [B]. On this later plane and respect to the first measurement, maximum wear is detected usually.
3. If the readings taken on wear, oval and conical eccentricities of cylinders are no greater than 0.4 mm in respect of the nominal Ø of the cylinders rectify cylinders accordingly

**Cylinder Specifications:**
- Nominal Ø of cylinders oversized by 0.4 mm: 94.802 ± 94.832 mm
- Standard cylinders Ø nominal diameter: 94.402 ± 94.432 mm
- Nominal Ø of pistons oversized by 0.4 mm: 94.706 ± 94.720 mm
- Nominal play between piston and cylinder: 0.082 ± 0.126 mm

**Important:** If measurements obtained concerning amount of wear, oval and conical shaping of cylinders are more than 0.4 mm when comparing against the nominal Ø of cylinders the block of cylinders should be recuperated with a lining up operation

**Cleaning After Machining:** Remove mechanization caps and clean thoroughly the block with cleaning fluid, compressed air and cylindrical sweeping brushes

### 5.2 Surface Coupling Measurement with Cylinder Head

**Procedure:**
1. Dismantle the fixtures [4]
2. Check flatness of upper face of the block using a metallic ruler [5] and a thickness calliper [6]

**Piston Overhanging Specifications:**
- Overhanging minimum and maximum of pistons in upper face of block: > 0.40 ± < 0.80 mm

**Important:** If lack of flatness is appreciated do a milling operation to achieve flatness ensuring that the overhanging of the pistons is within the specified range after milling. The cylinder head gasket comes in a kit of required gaskets to carry out complete repairs of the engine; this is the one with the greatest thickness. Obviously, it is also supplied separately according to required thickness.

### 5.3 Pistons Overhanging Control and Calculation of the Cylinder Head Gasket Thickness

**Piston Height Measurement:**
1. Check pistons overhanging with respect to the upper part of the cylinders block. To do this, use straightedge [E] and thickness gauge [F].

**Gasket Selection Table:**
| Average Pistons Overhanging | Cylinder Head Gasket Thickness |
|---|---|
| 0.61 ± 0.80 | 1.48 ± 1.62 (Free) / ~1.40 (Fit and adjusted) |
| 0.40 ± 0.60 | 1.23 ± 1.35 (Free) / ~1.20 (Fit and adjusted) |

## 6. Crankshaft Repair

### 6.1 Crankshaft Measurement

**Measurement of Journal Diameters:**
1. Using a micrometer [1] proceed to measure the journal of connecting rods [2] and main bearing [4] to determine the diameter to be reduced

**Important:** It is necessary to annotate the results of measurements in a table as the one herewith to find out the oversize to apply

**Journal Diameter Specifications (Nominal):**
- Connecting rod journal: 80.182 - 80.208 mm
- Main bearing journal: 56.515 - 56.538 mm

**Journal Diameter Specifications (Oversize):**
- Nominal value: 56.515 - 56.538 mm

### 6.2 Bearings of Main Bearing Installation

**Installation Procedure:**
1. Install the half bearings [3] on upper block (half ball bearings with greasing hole)

**Note:** Oversized ball bearings for main bearing are supplied with diameter reduced in -0.254 and -0.508 mm

**Play Between Bearings and Journals - Measuring:**
1. Place on each journal pieces of calibrated wire in the longitudinal direction
2. Length of calibrated wire must cover the width of the journal, approximately

**Important:** Do not turn the crankshaft once wire gauge has been installed

**Installation Play Between Bearings and Main Bearing Journals:** 0.032 ± 0.102 mm

### 6.3 Control of Crankshaft Axial Play

**Axial Play of Crankshaft Specification:** 0.060 ± 0.310 mm

**Important:** If axial play exceeds the tolerated range, substitute half ball bearings of the support n° 5

**If half ball bearings of the support n° 5 have been replaced, check again play of the installation between bearing and journal**

### 6.4 Main Bearing Clearance

**Procedure:**
1. Do as follows:
   - Clean carefully main journals and bearing
   - Put the main journals to be tested in the L.D.C. position, except number 4 which must be in T.D.C. position due to assembling difficulties of the bearing cap
   - Place a piece of wire gauge [27] to full width an journal [28] to be checked

**Important:** Do not turn crankshaft once wire gauge has been installed

**Main Bearing Clearance Specification:** 0.035 ± 0.083 mm

2. Install bearing caps [26] with bearings, lubricate the screws with oil and tighten
   - 1st step: 5 Kg-m (50 Nm)
   - 2nd step: 63° ± 2°

3. Remove caps [26] and check width of the calibrated wire gauge [27] at its widest point. For this, use engraved scale in envelope [29]

**Important:** The screws from caps will have to be always new when doing final installation

|---|---|---|

### 7.2 Checking the Gudgeon Pins of Pistons Installation

**Gudgeon Pin Diameter Specifications:** 31.994 ± 32.000 mm

**Play Between Bolt and Seating at the Piston:** 0.007 ± 0.019 mm

### 7.3 Connecting Rod Foot Bushes

**Internal Diameter for Rod Foot Connecting Bush:** 32.010 ± 32.020 mm

**Play Between Connecting Foot Bush and Gudgeon Pin of Piston:** 0.014 ± 0.030 mm

### 7.4 Checking the Play Between Piston Rings and Piston Slits or Grooves

**Piston Ring Thickness Specifications:**
- 1st piston rings: 2.068 ± 2.097 mm
- 2nd piston rings: 1.970 ± 1.995 mm
- 3rd piston rings: 2.470 ± 2.490 mm

**Play Between 2nd and 3rd Piston Rings and Piston Slits or Grooves:**
- 2nd piston rings: 0.055 ± 0.10 mm
- 3rd piston rings: 0.05 ± 0.09 mm

**Play Between Trapezoidal Piston Ring and Slit or Groove:** 0.103 ± 0.162 mm

**1st Piston Ring (Trapezoidal) Play Check Procedure:**
1. Install piston ring in the corresponding piston slit or groove piston
2. Fit piston in a cylinder as shown. Piston ring 1 must come out approximately one half by the upper part of cylinder [13]
3. In this position, measure play [X] using a thickness calliper. Play must be within specifications

**Distance Between Piston Rings Extremes:**
- 1st piston ring: 0.20 ± 0.35 mm
- 2nd piston ring: 0.30 ± 0.50 mm
- 3rd piston ring: 0.30 ± 0.55 mm

### 7.5 Connecting Rods Check

**Important Note:** Weight reduction of connecting rods by chipping out material is strongly no recommended

**Connecting Rod Specifications:**
1. Concerning connecting rods, noting the following remarks
   - A same number engraved in body and cover mean good matching between both
   - In cover, a letter engraved [0] or [X] means the diameter of the head chased for installation while produced in the car factory
   - In some cases, it can mean the installation order on the engine. When replacing connecting rod mark on the new one the installation order on the engine. This mark should be made in the opposite side of half ball bearings fixes
   - Connecting rods for replacements come with a diameter of head of 60.341 ± 60.348 mm marked with the letter [O] and the kind of weight marked with the number [33]
   - Connecting rods must all belong to the same category
   - Substitute all four connecting rods leven if there is only one with defects; keep only the one weighing the same than the ones supplied as spares

**Connecting Rod Head Diameter:** 60.341 ± 60.348 mm

### 7.6 Piston and Connecting Rod Assembly Installation on Cylinders

**Important:** Install each assembly constituted by the piston, connecting rod and connecting rod bearings, in, the same cylinder they were located before removal

1. Install piston and connecting rod assembly into corresponding cylinder hose noting the following remarks:
   - That number in connecting rod agrees with number in cover [25]
   - That end gaps of each piston ring are 120° out of phase from other two per ring
   - That upper engraved part [23] of each piston is oriented towards engine flywheel or that piston half open lip [22] found at the piston side is oriented towards oil injectors. Install assemblies constituted by piston and connecting

**When Using the Same Bearings Again Install Them in the Same Order and Position Than When They Were Removed**

**Valves Rod Diameter Specification:** 7.985 ± 8.000 mm

**Important:** If rods diameter does not adjust to specified values, substitute the valves

## 8. Torque Specifications for Engine Repair

**Cylinder Head Bolts:**
- 1st step: pretightening with torque wrench: **6 ± 0.5 Kg-m (60 ± 5 Nm)**
- 2nd step: pretightening with torque wrench: **6 ± 0.5 Kg-m (60 ± 5 Nm)**
- 3rd step: angular tightening: **180° ± 10°**

**Lower to Upper Block Union Bolts:**
- 1st step: pretightening with torque wrench: **5 ± 0.5 Kg-m (50 ± 5 Nm)**
- 2nd step: angular tightening: **90° ± 5°**

**Connecting Rods Caps Bolts:**
- 1st step: pretightening with torque wrench: **5 ± 0.5 Kg-m (50 ± 5 Nm)**
- 2nd step: angular tightening: **63° ± 2°**

**Flywheel Bolts:**
- 1st step: pretightening with torque wrench: **3 ± 0.3 Kg-m (30 ± 3 Nm)**
- 2nd step: angular tightening: **90° ± 2°**

**Fan Hub to Water Pump Selfbraking Nut:**
- 1st step: pretightening with torque wrench: **4.0 Kg-m (40 Nm)**
