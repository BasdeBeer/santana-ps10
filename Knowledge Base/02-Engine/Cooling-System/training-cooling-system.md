# Santana PS10 EIII - Cooling System Training Manual

**Engine:** Iveco 8140.43P (2.8 TD, Euro III)
**Source:** Dealer Training Manual - Cooling System (Pages 2-4)

---

## General Description

The refrigeration system of engine is of forced circulation type and is constituted by the following elements:

### System Components

- **Reservoir Tank** - Cap incorporates two valves:
  - One for emptying or flushing
  - One for filling

- **Radiator** - Dissipates heat that the cooling takes from the engine

- **Thermometric Switch** - Fitted in the base of radiator getting on and off an electromagnetic fan

- **Electromagnetic Fan** - Switching on when the temperature of coolant is 94 °C ± 2 °C and switching off at 80 °C ± 2 °C

- **Heat Exchanger** - Cools oil of engine

- **Centrifugal Type Water Pump** - Moved by the generator driving belt. In its axle incorporates the coupling bushing of the cooling fan

- **Three Way Thermostat** - Regulating the flow of the refrigerating liquid

---

## Running/Operation

### Thermostat Cold Startup

With a temperature of coolant below 79 °C ± 2 °C:

- The water pump moves around the coolant from the engine and the heating system refrigerating the cylinders block and cylinder head (thermostat closed)

### Thermostat Normal Operation

With a normal temperature of coolant (thermostat open):

- The thermostat coolant heated by engine to radiator. In this situation, the water pump moves all of system coolant

### Pressure Regulation

The pressure of circuit, due to the changes of temperature, is regulated by the valves (outlet and inlet) of reservoir tank.

**Outlet Valve Functions:**
- Keep the system slightly pressurized in such a way the boiling point of coolant
- Discharge outside the excess pressure produced when the coolant reaches too a high temperature

**Inlet Valve Function:**
- Allow the transfer of coolant from reservoir tank to the radiator when, within the system, there is a vacuum because decrease of coolant volume doesn't coolant temperature

**Valve Specifications:**
- Opening of flushing valve: 1 ± 0.1 Kg/cm²
- Opening of inlet valve: 0.005 ± 0.02 Kg/cm²

## Thermostat Operation Specifications

### Cold Start Condition
Below 79 °C ± 2 °C:
- Thermostat remains **closed**
- Coolant bypasses radiator through engine heating circuit
- Water pump circulates coolant through cylinder block and head only

### Normal Operating Temperature
Above 79 °C ± 2 °C:
- Thermostat **opens**
- Coolant flows through radiator for cooling
- Fan electromagnetic switch engages at 94 °C ± 2 °C
- Fan disengages at 80 °C ± 2 °C

### Pressure System Details

The cooling system is pressurized to raise the boiling point of the coolant and prevent cavitation.

**Reservoir Cap Valve Functions:**
- **Outlet (Relief) Valve**: Opens at 1 ± 0.1 Kg/cm² to prevent overpressurization
- **Inlet (Check) Valve**: Opens at 0.005 ± 0.02 Kg/cm² to allow makeup coolant from reservoir

**Maximum Thermostat Opening Temperature:** 110 °C

## Cooling System Maintenance

### Regular Inspection Items

1. **Visual Inspections**
   - Check hose condition and connections
   - Inspect radiator for debris/blockage
   - Check coolant level in reservoir (cold engine)
   - Look for coolant leaks at connections

2. **Coolant Checks**
   - Verify coolant type (usually ethylene glycol based)
   - Check concentration/freeze protection
   - Change coolant per maintenance schedule
   - Never mix different coolant types

3. **Component Verification**
   - Test thermostat operation
   - Verify electromagnetic fan engagement
   - Check water pump operation (no noise/vibration)
   - Inspect heat exchanger for blockage

### Service Intervals

| Item | Interval (KM) | Interval (Miles) | Interval (Months) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Water Radiator and Air Radiator (Intercooler) Inspection | 10,000 | 6,000 | 8 |

## Coolant Specifications

### Recommended Type
- **Standard**: Ethylene glycol based antifreeze/coolant
- **Protection Level**: From -30 °C to +110 °C

### System Capacity
- Varies depending on configuration
- Refer to service manual for exact capacity
- Flush system completely during coolant change

### Thermal Fan Engagement

The electromagnetic fan is thermostat controlled:
- **Engagement Temperature**: 94 °C ± 2 °C
- **Disengagement Temperature**: 80 °C ± 2 °C
- **Fan Type**: Electromagnetic with integrated bushing

---

## Thermostat Replacement Notes

- Maximum opening temperature: 110 °C
- Thermostat is three-way type with bypass function
- Must be properly seated in housing
- Always use new gaskets/seals when replacing
- Bleed system of air after thermostat replacement
- Verify proper coolant flow after installation

---

## System Pressure Relief

The radiator cap serves critical safety functions:

1. **Maintains System Pressure** - Elevates coolant boiling point
2. **Prevents Overpressure** - Relief valve opens if pressure exceeds limit
3. **Allows Makeup Coolant** - Check valve permits refilling from reservoir

**Critical Safety Note:** Never open radiator cap on hot engine to prevent:
- Sudden pressure release
- Boiling coolant spray
- Severe burns

Allow engine to cool before servicing the cooling system.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Possible Cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Overheating | Thermostat stuck closed, fan failure, blockage | Inspect thermostat, test fan, flush radiator |
| Coolant Leaks | Deteriorated hoses, loose connections, pump seal | Inspect and replace hoses, tighten fittings |
| Low Coolant Level | Leaks, evaporation | Check for leaks, refill with correct coolant |
| Cold Engine | Thermostat stuck open, sensor failure | Replace thermostat, test temperature sensor |
| Fan Always On | Thermostat stuck open, sensor malfunction | Diagnose sensor/thermostat |
| Noise/Vibration | Water pump bearing wear | Replace water pump |

---

## Safety Precautions

1. **Never remove radiator cap on a hot engine**
2. **Check coolant level only when engine is cold**
3. **Use proper type of coolant for this engine**
4. **Do not overfill the reservoir tank**
5. **Ensure proper air bleeding after service**
6. **Dispose of old coolant properly** - It is toxic
