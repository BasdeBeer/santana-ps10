# Complete Brake System Documentation

**Source:** Service Manual PS10.pdf (pages 175-210) and Rear Brakes 5-29.pdf

## System Overview

### Brake System Architecture (Service Manual PS10, page 5-4)

The brake system uses a **tandem master cylinder with two independent circuits**:
- Primary chamber ("a") acts on rear wheels brakes
- Secondary chamber ("b") acts on front wheels brakes

The system includes:
- Vacuum pump (for brake booster assistance)
- Brake booster assembly
- Load Sensing Pressure Valve (LSPV) - controls rear brake pressure based on vehicle loading
- Front and rear brake calipers with disc brakes
- Master cylinder with integrated check valves
- Parking brake system (mechanical drum type in transfer box)

### Brake Caliper Types (Service Manual PS10, page 5-4)

**Front brake caliper:** Two pistons
**Rear brake caliper:** Single piston

Both operate via hydraulic pressure acting on pistons to force brake pads against discs.

## Brake Components - Detailed Descriptions

### Master Cylinder Assembly (Service Manual PS10, page 5-5)

**Location:** Mounted on brake booster assembly

**Construction:**
- Two pistons and three oil-seals (leathers) in each master cylinder
- Hydraulic pressure produced in primary chamber ("a") acts on rear wheels brakes
- Hydraulic pressure in secondary chamber ("b") acts on front wheels brakes
- Contains integral check valves and pressure regulation

**Key Note (Service Manual PS10, page 5-5):**
- Replace all components included in repair kits to service the master cylinder
- Lubricate rubber parts with clean, fresh brake fluid only
- Do not use shop air on brake parts (damage to rubber components)
- If any hydraulic component is removed or line disconnected, bleed the brake system
- All replacement pads in axle sets only
- Torque values specified are for dry, unlubricated fasteners

**Operation (Service Manual PS10, page 5-5):**
When brake pedal is pressed, primary piston "A" moves toward the left, generating hydraulic pressure in chamber "a". Through pressure and springing return force, secondary piston "B" also moves left, creating hydraulic pressure in chamber "b".

### Vacuum Pump (Service Manual PS10, page 5-6)

**Location:** Auxiliary organs group

**Function:** Provides vacuum pressure continuously to brake booster, working as a tandem system to decrease physical effort required during braking. Vacuum pressure is transmitted to brake booster which converts it to linear motion applied to master cylinder rod.

### Brake Booster Assembly (Service Manual PS10, page 5-7)

**Operation:**
- Vacuum pressure acts on chambers "A" and "B" of brake booster while brake pedal and control valve "C" stand still
- This position allows communication between both chambers and avoids external air entrance to chamber "B"
- When brake pedal is pressed, control valve "C" closes communication between chambers "A" and "B"
- This allows entrance of external air to chamber "B"
- All generated vacuum pressure by vacuum pump system gets into chamber "A" (at master cylinder side)
- This leads to displacement towards this side of membrane "D"
- Pushing rod "E" which drives brake master cylinder pistons, producing necessary hydraulic pressure

### Load Sensing Pressure Valve (LSPV) (Service Manual PS10, page 5-8)

**Function:** Controls hydraulic pressure applied to rear wheels brakes as a function of vehicle loading, avoiding rear wheels lock-up during braking. Frees previously described control on hydraulic pressure applied to rear wheels brakes if there is any failure in front wheel hydraulic braking circuit. Applies hydraulic pressure produced in master cylinder directly to activate rear wheels brakes.

**Location:** Vehicle framework, with lever end connected to rear axle housing through sensing spring and support of LSPV.

**Operation:**

*Vacuum state:*
Since sensor spring is attracted by relatively weak force, force applied to piston is weak and hydraulic characteristic curve lies low.

*Loaded state:*
Since sensor spring is attracted by relatively strong force, applied force to pistons is greater and hydraulic characteristic curve lies higher.

**Installation Details (Service Manual PS10, page 5-33):**
- LSPV assembly must not be disassembled; replace with new one if defective
- Spring connections require recommended grease (Grease AGUILA 95, ref. 790999)

**Adjustment (Service Manual PS10, page 5-34):**
- Spring length (L): 150 ± 1 mm
- Measured with finger pressing spring connect screw at finger contact of screw
- If spring length differs from specification, adjust by turning adjustment screw eccentrically head of screw
- Tighten adjustment nut to specified torque afterward

## On-Vehicle Service Procedures

### Brake Pedal Free Height Adjustment (Service Manual PS10, page 5-12)

**Free height "A" of brake pedal:** approximately 105 mm

**Procedure:**
1. Check brake pedal free height
2. If not within specification, adjust lunge fork length

**Fork length adjustment (Service Manual PS10, page 5-12):**
1. Remove nuts (2) fixing master cylinder (1) and take away master cylinder assembly and fluid tank
2. Disconnect vacuum tube (3) from connection to lung (4)
3. Remove rubber cap of brake pedal support and disconnect fork (5) from connection to pedal
4. Withdraw lung (4) dismantling nuts (6) fixing to pedal support
5. Check distance (B) of fork length which must be within specified values

**Fork adjustment length (B):** 147.5 ±0.5 mm

6. Adjust length acting on fork counter nut and fix position with counter nut
7. Reverse lung and master cylinder procedures noting specified tightening torques

**Tightening torques (Service Manual PS10, page 5-12):**
- Nuts fixing lung to pedal support: 1 + 1.6 Kg-m (10 + 16 Nm)
- Nuts fixing master cylinder to lung: 1 + 1.6 Kg-m (10 + 16 Nm)

### Stoplight Switch Adjustment (Service Manual PS10, page 5-13)

**Play (A) of stopping switch:** 0.5 ± 1 mm

**Procedure:**
1. Push brake pedal towards you and keep it like that
2. Adjust switch position such that play between thread end and brake pedal top lies between specified values
3. Once play has been checked and adjusted, tighten counter nut to specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for counter nut of stopping switch:** 1 + 1.5 Kg-m (10 + 15 Nm)

### Brake Pedal Path Revision (Service Manual PS10, page 5-13)

**Play (A) of pedal in braking position:** approximately 71 mm

**Procedure:**
1. Start engine
2. Depress brake pedal a few times
3. Depress brake pedal with about 30 kg load and check play (A) between brake pedal and flooring

**If play is less than specification (Service Manual PS10, page 5-13):**
- Air may be present in brake system conductions - purge the circuit (see 5-17)
- Maladjustment in brake pedal height - adjust acting on brake booster lung fork (see 5-12)

### Master Cylinder Inspection (Service Manual PS10, page 5-13)

**Procedure:**
1. Check for cracked master cylinder casting or brake fluid around master cylinder
2. Estimate any leaks when there is even a drop of fluid - anything damp is thought abnormal
3. If any type of leak is detected, substitute master cylinder (see 5-31)

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-13):**
Master cylinder should not be repaired. When working anomalies or fluid leaks are detected, it must be substituted.

### Brake Discs Inspection (Service Manual PS10, page 5-14)

**Procedure:**
1. Remove wheel (see 3F-4)
2. Inspect brake disc surface for scratches in wearing parts

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-14):**
Scratches on disc surface are normal and disc is not defective unless they are very deep.

3. If scratches or scratches all over disc surface, substitute the brake disc (see 5-29)
4. When only one side is scratched, polis and correct that side without exceeding servicing limit

**Brake disc thickness specifications (Service Manual PS10, page 5-30):**

| Type | Standard | Service limit |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Front | 20 mm | 18 mm |
| Rear | 10 mm | 9 mm |

**Deflection measurement (Service Manual PS10, page 5-14):**
To measure brake disc deflection, take readings in two points located at border and at centre of brake disc with dial gauge fixed to housing and with tip measuring tip touching the disc.

**Limit on brake disc deflection:** 0.15 mm

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-14):**
When measuring, check for looseness at wheel bearing.

6. Install wheel (see 3F-4)

### Brake Pad Inspection (Service Manual PS10, page 5-14)

**Procedure:**
Inspect periodically thickness of pads according to 0B, MAINTENANCE AND LUBRICACION (0B-21), and every time wheel get removed, looking always through each end (or hole) of caliper.

**Brake pad thickness specifications (Service Manual PS10, page 5-14):**

| Type | Standard | Servicing limit |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| Front | 15.30 mm | 8 mm |
| Rear | 15.60 mm | 8.2 mm |

## Service Procedures

### Brake Disc Substitution (Service Manual PS10, page 5-29 and Rear Brakes 5-29.pdf)

#### Removal

1. Remove wheel (see section 3F-4 of Service Manual)
2. Disconnect brake caliper (1) from its support, taking it away from hose and remove support (2)

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-29):**
- Do not act on brake pedal till new brake caliper has been fully installed
- While carrying out this task, brake caliper must be kept out-of-the-way adequately; do not allow it to stay hanging from brake hose

3. Install dial gauge fixing it to axle housing and with measuring tip resting at about 2 mm from outer edge of disc
4. Rotate disc and check deflection - there should be no deflection higher than indicated

**Brake disc maximum deflection:** 0.15 mm (Service Manual PS10, page 5-29 and Rear Brakes 5-29.pdf)

5. If deviation is higher, mechanically rectify the brake disc without exceeding service limit
6. Remove driving flange (3)
7. Loosen screws (4) joining brake disc to hub
8. Strike brake disc and hub assembly (see section 4A-13)

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-29):**
Hold disc tightly in a vice, use breaker bar. These are very tight with thread lock applied at factory. They need to be wound out completely.

9. Draw to aligning mark (A) on disc and hub and separate them

**Tip:** Use two pairs of 10 mm bolts (2nd pair longer than the first) to jack the disc off the hub

#### Installation (Service Manual PS10, page 5-30)

1. Align marks (A) you did under task 9 and fix brake disc to hub tightening screws to specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for nuts fixing brake disc:** 3.5 Kg-m (35 Nm) (Service Manual PS10, page 5-30)

2. Proceed to install hub and to its adjustment (see 4A-13)
3. Install support (2) of brake caliper and caliper (1) tightening screws at specified tightening torque

**Tightening torques (Service Manual PS10, page 5-30):**
- Screws fixing brake caliper support: 6.5 + 7.5 Kg-m (65 + 75 Nm)
- Screws fixing brake caliper: 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)

#### Rear Brake Disc Installation Notes (Rear Brakes 5-29.pdf)

- Clean hub thoroughly of rust and debris with wire brush so new disc runs true with hub face
- Recommended: set up hub in lathe, check with DTI (Dial Test Indicator), fit new disc, and re-check with DTI
- Reverse removal procedure to install back on vehicle
- Use of thread lock on Allen bolts is at owner's discretion

#### Key Rear Brake Disc Specifications (Rear Brakes 5-29.pdf)

- Hub nut size: 2" / approximately 50 mm AF
- Disc-to-hub bolts: 6 Allen key bolts
- Max disc deflection (runout): 0.15 mm

### Brake Disc Assembly Repair and Inspection (Service Manual PS10, page 5-24)

#### Pads Substitution (Service Manual PS10, page 5-24)

1. Loosen wheel nuts
2. Lift vehicle and remove wheel
3. Remove brake caliper fixing screws (1) from connection to support (2) and pull them apart without forcing hose

**Tightening torque for screws fixing clamp:** 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)

4. Substitute pads (3) by new ones
5. Install caliper (1) in support (2) and tighten screws to specified tightening torque
6. Install wheel and fit tightening nuts
7. Bring down vehicle and tighten wheel fixing nuts to specified tightening in sequence order shown

**Tightening torque for wheel nuts:** 11 + 17 Kg-m (110 + 170 Nm) (Service Manual PS10, page 5-24)

### Parking Brake Lever and Cable Substitution (Service Manual PS10, page 5-23)

#### Lever Substitution

1. Remove lower and central profiles from right door and central side respectively and lift partly flooring carpet to have access to parking brake lever
2. Disconnect electric connector from parking brake activating switch
3. Take away counter nut of parking brake cable
4. Remove parking brake lever fixing screws
5. Remove pin and remove pin fixing cable to lever
6. Substitute lever for a new one
7. Install other components reversing removal procedures
8. Once lever is fitted, proceed to adjust parking brake (see 5-15)

#### Cable Substitution

1. As formerly done, lift flooring carpet to have access to parking brake lever and disconnect cable
2. From lower part, take away retention clip and remove pin fixing cable to cam
3. Substitute cable for new one
4. Install components reversing removal procedures
5. Once cable and lever have been fitted, proceed to adjust parking brake (see 5-15)

### Parking Brake Inspection and Adjustment (Service Manual PS10, page 5-15)

#### Inspection

1. Pull slowly from parking brake lever and check maximum efficiency is found at ratchet number 7

**Maximum handbrake efficiency:** Point 7 of the ratchet (Service Manual PS10, page 5-15)

2. Check that both rear wheels are firmly blocked
3. If ratchet number does not adjust to specified one, proceed to adjust parking brake cable

#### Adjustment At the Drum

1. Bring up brake drum shoes to transmission till they friction with brake drum acting on tensioning screw (1). Then unscrew one turn the screw to free brake drum

2. Adjust parking brake cable acting on drum fork (2) and fix it with counter nut

3. Install all components reversing removal procedures

#### Adjustment In the Lever

1. Remove right front seat (see 9-27)
2. Remove protecting profiles from front (3) and rear doors (4) of right side of vehicle
3. Lift partly front carpet to accede to parking brake cable tensioning mechanism located in parking brake lever
4. Loosen or tighten tensioning nut from parking brake cable to achieve indicated maximum efficiency

**Parking brake maximum efficiency:** Point 7 of the ratchet (Service Manual PS10, page 5-16)

5. Install all components reversing removal procedures

### Brake Caliper Assembly Substitution (Service Manual PS10, page 5-25)

1. Remove wheel
2. Install a Girling clamp (A) ref. 181661 in brake hose (1) to strangle brake fluid falling out
3. Remove hose from connection to brake caliper

4. Remove screws (2) fixing brake caliper to support and extract it
5. Substitute brake pads if necessary
6. Install new brake caliper and fix it with screws to specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for screws fixing brake clamp:** 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)

7. Install brake hose, fixing it at specified tightening torque and withdraw strangulation Girling clamp

**Tightening torque for fixing brake sleeve to brake clamp:** 2 + 2.5 Kg-m (20 + 25 Nm)

8. Once brake caliper assembly has been installed, carry out purging of brakes system (see 5-17) and check there are no leaks
9. Install wheel (see 3F-4) and check brakes on road

### Brake Caliper Assembly Repair (Service Manual PS10, page 5-26)

#### Removal

1. Remove wheel (see 3F-4)
2. Remove brake caliper (see 5-25)

#### Repair - Disassembly

1. Place rag cushion in front of pistons to avoid they get damaged
2. Remove pistons using compressed air at moderate pressure

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-26):**
Do not put your hands where pistons could damage them when coming out suddenly.

3. Remove dust cover
4. Remove oil-seals (1) from pistons, using thin blade like thickness gauge and paying attention not to damage cylinders

#### Repair - Revision

5. Verify dust covers in case there are any breakages, cracks or damages, and replace if necessary
6. Verify oil-seals in case they show deformations, wear, decomposition, etc.

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-27):**
Excessive wear on pads could be caused by faulty return of piston. In such case, change oil-seals.

7. Verify worthiness of cylinders and pistons in case they show wears, scratches, corrosion, etc. Substitute them if necessary
8. Ensure purge hose is not blocked

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-27):**
To wash metallic parts, use a solution of hot water and neutral detergent.

#### Repair - Assembly

9. Lubricate cylinders, pistons and oil-seals with new brake fluid
10. Install oil-seals in cylinders such a way they are perfectly fitted in channelings
11. Place dust covers on pistons, ensuring they stay positioned correctly as shown in figure

12. Install piston with their corresponding cylinders and fix dust cover clip in cylinder internal channeling
13. Introduce thoroughly pistons and ensuring that dust cover clip is lodged in piston channeling

#### Installation

14. Reverse removal procedures, noting specified tightening torques in complementary tasks

### Brake Hose and Pipe Substitution (Service Manual PS10, page 5-22)

1. Place vehicle in appropriate working ground
2. Clean dirt and accumulated impurities at end of pipes, brake hoses and surrounding
3. Dismantle pipes or hoses plugging holes to pour out minimum possible amount of fluid

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-22):**
Brake fluid is extremely damaging to paint. If fluid should accidentally touch painted surface, immediately wipe from paint and clean painted surface.

4. Install new pipes or hose, noting following points:
   - Place correctly clips or braces
   - Be sure hose is not twisted neither trapped
   - Be sure hose is no contact other parts which could lead to rubbing rubbings
   - Check hose and pipes under steering maximum rotation condition, right and left. If there are contacts, remove and correct
   - Supply and keep adequate level of brake fluid in tank

5. Proceed to purge braking system (see 5-17)

### Brake Fluid Substitution and Circuit Purging (Service Manual PS10, page 5-17 to 5-19)

#### Fluid Substitution

1. Attach vinyl tube (1) with end connected to brake caliper purger (2) (of one of four wheels) and other end introduced in empty container
2. Press few times brake pedal and keeping it pressed, loose purging screw (2)
3. Keep pressed brake pedal till circuit lose pressure

4. Fill tank (3) with new recommended fluid and keep it half full substitution of fluid is carried out
5. Remove fluid through wheel purger till new fluid comes out
6. Do above task in other wheels
7. When brake fluid has been replaced, proceed to purging circuit

#### Circuit Purging

1. Fill tank (3) with new brake fluid and keep it half full while purging

2. Attach vinyl tube (1) with end connected to brake caliper purger (2) (one of four wheels) and other end introduced in appropriate empty to pour fluid

3. Press few times brake pedal and keeping it pressed, loose purging screw (2) turning it between one third and full turn

4. When fluid pressure is about nil, tighten purging screw (2)
5. Repeat this task till air bubbles disappear from hydraulic pipes

6. When bubbles stop coming, press brake pedal and keep it pressed, tighten moderately purging screw (2)

7. Assemble purging protection cap (4)
8. Install pressure in circuit and check for leaks
9. Carry out purging task in rest of wheels

10. Fill again tank with new recommended fluid till specified maximum level (MAX)

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-18):**
- Do not use again replaced fluid neither the one used in air purging operation
- Use always new recommended brake fluid

11. Check if brake pedal feels soft; if this is so, repeat again brake fluid purging procedure

### Master Cylinder Purging (Service Manual PS10, page 5-19)

1. Fill brake fluid tank (1) with new recommended fluid and wait at least one minute
2. Disconnect pipe (2) from primary side (of rear wheels)
3. Press slowly brake pedal
4. Block hole with finger and release gradually brake pedal, keeping it closed for about five seconds before pressing again brake pedal
5. Repeat former procedure till fluid comes out through hole
6. Repeat again procedure three times at least and connect pipe (2)

7. Disconnect pipes (3) and (4) from secondary side (of front wheel)
8. With holes blocked with fingers, carry out purging as described before (Task 4)
9. Once purging is finished, connect again both pipes
10. Proceed to purge brake system (see 5-17)

### Vacuum Circuit Checking (Service Manual PS10, page 5-20 to 5-21)

#### Circuit Checking

1. Do trial on road and verify that help towards braking is enough
2. If help towards braking not satisfactory, disconnect sleeve (2) from brake booster (3) connection and fix vacuum meter in series point (A)

3. With engine at 1000 rpm, check if press vacuum reading is near to specified

**Vacuum pressure:** 850 mbar (Service Manual PS10, page 5-20)

**Otherwise, check (Service Manual PS10, page 5-20):**
- Eventual leaks through vacuum sleeve (2)
- Eventual blockage of depressor exit (1) or breakdown in it
- Eventual leaks in brake booster (3)
- Eventual leaks in vacuum tank (4)

#### Locating Leaks and Breakdowns

4. Connect vacuum meter to vacuum pump and carry out checking as explained in task 3

5. If reading is not correct, depressor is faulty. If it is right then do as follows (Service Manual PS10, page 5-21):
   - Connect sleeve (2) to depressor (1)
   - Disconnect sleeve (2) from connection to brake booster (3) and fit vacuum meter at free end
   - Carry out checking as explained in task 3

6. If reading is right, tank is faulty; otherwise sleeve is faulty (Service Manual PS10, page 5-21)

7. If reading is right, tank is faulty; otherwise sleeve is faulty (Service Manual PS10, page 5-21)

8. One faulty component has been substituted, check again circuit; if reading not right, brake booster is faulty too

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-21):**
In practice, to carry out brake booster checking, press slightly brake pedal till finding initial resistance (engine off). Keeping brake pedal in this position, start engine and check that with same pressure brake pedal goes down progressively; otherwise brake booster is faulty.

### Brake Booster Assembly Inspection (Service Manual PS10, page 5-32)

#### Brake Booster Vacuum Pump Substitution

For removal and installation of vacuum pump or depressor, see 6A-65.

#### Brake Booster Lung Substitution

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-32):**
Brake booster lung allows no repair. When anomalous behaviour is detected, replace the whole ensemble.

##### Removal

1. Remove brake fluid from tank by using syringe or similar, disconnect three brake pipes (2) and remove nuts connecting master cylinder (1) to lung and withdraw master cylinder assembly and tank

2. Disconnect vacuum pipe (2) from connection to lung (3)
3. Remove rubber caps (4) from brake pedal support and disconnect fork (5)
4. Withdraw lung (3) remove nuts (6) fixing to brake pedal support
5. Substitute brake booster lung by a new one

##### Installation

1. On new lung (3), adjust distance (B) by using fork (5) and fix fork with their locknut. This distance must agree with one of removed lung

**Fork adjusting distance (B):** 147.5±0.5 mm (Service Manual PS10, page 5-32)

2. Continue installation reversing removal procedures the specified tightening torques

**Tightening torques (Service Manual PS10, page 5-32):**
- Screws fixing brake booster lung to support: 1+1.6 Kg-m (10+16 Nm)
- Brake pipes linkages: 1.4+1.8 Kg-m (14+18 Nm)

### LSPV Removal, Installation and Adjustment (Service Manual PS10, page 5-33 to 5-34)

#### Removal

1. Clean around brake fluid tank cap and remove fluid with syringe or similar
2. Place vehicle in appropriate working ground
3. Disconnect brake pipes from LSPV (1)
4. Remove screws (2) fixing LSPV (1) and take support (4)
5. Withdraw LSPV assembly (1), spring (3) and its support (4)
6. Disconnect spring (3) from LSPV lever

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-33):**
- Do not loosen any screws or nuts from LSPV assembly indicated in figure
- LSPV assembly must not be disassembled; replace with new one if defective

#### Installation

1. Install LSPV reversing removal procedures noting following remarks and tightening torques

**Apply recommended grease in upper and lower connections (5) of spring (3) - Grease AGUILA 95, ref. 790999 (Service Manual PS10, page 5-33)**

**Tightening torques (Service Manual PS10, page 5-34):**
- Screws fixing valve LSPV: 1.8 + 2.8 Kg-m (18 + 28 Nm)
- Fixing screws of support: 1.8 + 2.8 Kg-m (18 + 28 Nm)
- Purger: 1+1.3 Kg-m (10 + 13 Nm)
- Linkages fixing brake pipes: 1.4+1.8 Kg-m (14 + 18 Nm)

2. Fill tank with recommended fluid and purge air from brake system (see 5-17)

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-34):**
Make sure to purge air form LSPV purger without fail

3. Check, after purging, if LSPV is properly installed and adjusted

#### Adjustments

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-34):**
Before adjustment and inspection check before hand the following points:
- Fuel tank is full
- Spare wheel, tools, and jack and its crank are on vehicle
- Vehicle has no other type of loading

##### Spring Length Adjustment

1. Place vehicle on levelled ground
2. Push LSPV lever with finger till contact of screw and measure length (L) of helicoid spring (3) which must correspond with specified value

**Spring length (L):** 150 ± 1 mm (Service Manual PS10, page 5-34)

3. If spring length (L) is different than this value then adjust length (L) turning eccentrically head of screw (5); tighten afterwards nut (6) to specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for nut fixing adjustment:** 1.8 + 2.8 Kg-m (18 + 28 Nm) (Service Manual PS10, page 5-34)

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-34):**
Check to make sure that LSPV body and brake pipes are free form fluid leakage. Replace defective parts, if any.

### Master Cylinder Substitution (Service Manual PS10, page 5-31)

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-31):**
The master cylinder does not allow any repairs. If abnormal behaviour or fluid leaks are found then substitute assembly.

1. Disconnect master cylinder from brake three pipes (2) and plug them to avoid fluid leaks

**Important (Service Manual PS10, page 5-31):**
Brake fluid is extremely damaging to paint. Do not allow brake fluid to get on painted surfaces.

2. Remove nuts fixing cylinder and take it away
3. Clean coupling area and fit new master cylinder tightening nuts to specified tightening torque

**Tightening torque for nuts fixing master cylinder:** 1 + 1.6 Kg-m (10 + 16 Nm) (Service Manual PS10, page 5-35)

4. Proceed to install reversing removal procedure, carry out master cylinder purging (see 5-19)
5. Connect brake tubes (2), tighten linkages to specified tightening torque and purge circuit (see 5-17)

**Tightening torque for linkages fixing brake pipes to master cylinder:** 1.4 + 1.8 Kg-m (14 + 18 Nm) (Service Manual PS10, page 5-35)

## Diagnostics and Troubleshooting

**Source:** Service Manual PS10.pdf, pages 5-9 to 5-11

### Road Testing Brakes (Service Manual PS10, page 5-9)

Brakes should be tested on dry, clean, smooth and reasonably level roadway which is not crowned. Road test brakes by making brake applications with both light and heavy pedal forces at various speeds to determine if vehicle stops evenly and effectively. Also drive vehicle to see if it leads to one side or the without brake application. If it does, check tire pressure, front end alignment and front suspension attachments for looseness. See diagnosis table for other causes.

### Brake Fluid Leaks (Service Manual PS10, page 5-9)

Check master cylinder fluid levels. While a slight drop in reservoir level does result from normal lining wear, an abnormally low level indicates leak in system. In such case, check entire brake system for leakage. If even slight evidence of leakage is noted, cause should be corrected or defective parts should be replaced. If fluid level is lower than minimum level of reservoir, refilling is necessary. Fill reservoir with specified brake fluid.

### Substandard or Contaminated Brake Fluid (Service Manual PS10, page 5-9)

Improper brake fluid, mineral oil or water in fluid may cause brake fluid to boil or rubber components in hydraulic system to deteriorate.

If primary piston cups are swollen, rubber parts have deteriorated. This deterioration may be evidenced by swollen wheel cylinder piston cups on drum brake wheels.

If deterioration of rubber is evident, desassemble all hydraulic parts and wash with alcohol. Dry these parts with compressed air before assembly to keep alcohol out of system. Replace all rubber parts in this system, including hoses. Also, when working on brake mechanisms, check for fluid on linings. If excessive fluid is found, replace linings.

If master cylinder piston seals are satisfactory, check for leakage or excessive heat conditions. If condition not found, drain fluid, flush with brake fluid, refill and bleed system.

The system must be flushed if there is any doubt as to grade of fluid in system or if fluid has been used which contained parts that have been subjected to contaminated fluid.
