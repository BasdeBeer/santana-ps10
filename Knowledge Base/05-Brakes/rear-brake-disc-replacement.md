# Rear Brake Disc Substitution

**Source:** Rear Brakes 5-29.pdf (community/workshop guide with photos)

## Removal

1. Remove wheel (see section 3F-4 of Service Manual)
2. Disconnect brake caliper (1) from its support, taking it away from hose and remove support (2)
   - **CAUTION:** Do not press the brake pedal until the new brake caliper has been fully installed
   - **CAUTION:** The brake caliper must be kept out-of-the-way adequately; do not allow it to stay hanging from the brake hose
3. Install a dial gauge fixing it to the axle housing, measuring tip resting at about 2mm from the outer edge of the disc
4. Rotate the disc and check deflection
   - **Brake disc maximum deflection: 0.15 mm**
5. If deviation is higher, mechanically rectify the disc without exceeding the service limit
6. Remove driving flange and half shaft (6 hex head bolts, item 3 on drawing), lay aside
7. Remove hub:
   - (a) Fold back locking tabs on bearing/hub retaining nut
   - (b) Remove two bearing hub nuts (2" / 50mm AF approx)
   - (c) Remove outer bearing roller race and thrust washer, noting position for replacement
   - (d) Remove hub and disc
8. Strike/mark disc and hub (required when fitting a new disc)
9. Remove 6 Allen key bolts from disc
   - **Tip:** Hold disc tightly in a vice, use a breaker bar. These are very tight with thread lock applied at factory. They need to be wound out completely
10. Separate the disc from the hub
    - **Tip:** Use two pairs of 10mm bolts (2nd pair longer than the first) to jack the disc off the hub

## Installation

- Clean the hub thoroughly of rust and debris with a wire brush so the new disc runs true with the hub face
- Recommended: set up the hub in a lathe, check with DTI (Dial Test Indicator), fit the new disc, and re-check with DTI
- Reverse the removal procedure to install back on vehicle
- Use of thread lock on Allen bolts is at owner's discretion

## Key Specs
- Hub nut size: 2" / ~50mm AF
- Disc-to-hub bolts: 6x Allen key bolts
- Max disc deflection (runout): **0.15 mm**
