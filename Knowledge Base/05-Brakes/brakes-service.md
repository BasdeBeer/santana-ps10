# Section 5: Brakes

## Overview

This section covers the service procedures for the complete brake system of the Santana PS10 vehicle, including brake diagnostics, disc and pad inspection, parking brake operation, fluid substitution, master cylinder maintenance, vacuum circuits, brake pipes and hoses, and caliper assembly procedures. The brake system is a critical safety component and all service procedures must be followed precisely.

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** The brake system is a safety-critical system. All service procedures must be performed according to specification. Any incorrect assembly or adjustment could result in brake failure and serious safety hazards.

## Brake System Diagnostics and Inspection

### General Diagnostics Procedure

Brake system failures can manifest as multiple symptoms. When diagnosing brake issues, refer to CHARTS OF GENERAL DIAGNOSTICS (3-2-2) for comprehensive failure mode analysis.

**Initial Inspection Steps:**

1. Visual inspection of brake fluid level in master cylinder reservoir
2. Check brake pedal travel and firmness
3. Inspect all brake lines for leaks or damage
4. Check brake pad wear indicators
5. Test brake fluid condition (color, clarity, moisture content)
6. Verify parking brake holding capability
7. Inspect brake discs for wear, scoring, or warping

### Brake Inspection Specifications

The following specifications define acceptable brake component condition:

**Brake Disc Specifications:**
- Standard thickness (front): 20 mm
- Standard thickness (rear): 10 mm
- Servicing limit (front): 18 mm
- Servicing limit (rear): 9 mm
- Maximum disc runout: 0.15 mm
- Surface finish: Smooth, free from scoring or heavy rust

**Brake Pad Specifications:**
- Standard thickness (front): 15.30 mm
- Standard thickness (rear): 15.60 mm
- Servicing limit (front): 8 mm
- Servicing limit (rear): 8.2 mm
- Wear indicators: Some pads equipped with audible wear indicators

**Action Required:**
- Replace brake discs if worn beyond servicing limits
- Replace brake pads if worn to servicing limit or showing uneven wear
- Inspect caliper pistons for proper operation
- Check for brake fluid leakage at all connection points

## Brake Disc Inspection and Replacement

### Front Brake Disc Inspection

**Inspection Procedure:**

1. Place vehicle on lift or appropriate support structure
2. Remove front wheel (see Section 3F-4 for wheel removal)
3. Visually inspect disc for:
   - Thickness measurement in at least 3 locations (use micrometer)
   - Surface condition (scoring, rust, glazing)
   - Run-out condition (use dial indicator mounted on caliper bracket)

**Disc Runout Measurement:**
- Mount dial indicator with probe contacting disc friction surface
- Rotate disc slowly through complete revolution
- Record maximum runout reading
- Replace disc if runout exceeds 0.15 mm

### Rear Brake Disc Inspection

**Inspection Procedure:**

1. Place vehicle on lift or appropriate support structure
2. Remove rear wheel (see Section 3F-4)
3. Remove brake caliper (see brake caliper section below)
4. Remove disc by unbolting from axle flange (typically 4 bolts)
5. Measure thickness in at least 3 locations:
   - Inner area
   - Middle area
   - Outer area

**Disc Replacement Decision:**
- If any measurement is below 9 mm servicing limit, replace disc
- If runout exceeds 0.15 mm, replace disc
- If heavy scoring or glazing is observed, replace disc

### Brake Disc Replacement Procedure

**Removal:**

1. Remove wheel using proper wheel removal procedure
2. Remove brake caliper (see caliper section)
3. Support caliper with wire to prevent hanging from brake hose
4. Remove disc retaining bolts (typically 4 bolts on rear; front disc may be integral with hub)
5. Withdraw disc from axle flange

**Installation:**

1. Clean axle flange mating surface thoroughly
2. Install new disc and secure with retaining bolts (if applicable)
3. Install brake caliper and tighten to specification (see caliper torque specifications)
4. Pump brake pedal to establish brake pad contact with disc
5. Install wheel and tighten to specification (see wheel torque specifications)

**Torque Specifications:**
- Brake disc bolts (rear): 4 + 5 Kg-m (40 + 50 Nm)
- Wheel nuts: 11 + 17 Kg-m (110 + 170 Nm)

## Brake Pad Inspection and Replacement

### Front Brake Pad Inspection

**Visual Inspection:**

1. Place vehicle on lift with front wheels off ground
2. Without removing wheel, visually inspect pad thickness through wheel openings
3. If wear indicators are visible or audible clicking occurs during braking, pads require replacement
4. Check for uneven wear (inner vs. outer pad)

**Specification Check:**
- Standard thickness: 15.30 mm
- Servicing limit: 8 mm
- Replace if any pad is at or below 8 mm

### Rear Brake Pad Inspection

**Visual Inspection:**

1. Place vehicle on lift with rear wheels off ground
2. Visually inspect pad condition through wheel opening
3. Check pad thickness using visual wear indicators

**Specification Check:**
- Standard thickness: 15.60 mm
- Servicing limit: 8.2 mm
- Replace if any pad is at or below 8.2 mm

### Brake Pad Replacement Procedure

**Front Brake Pad Replacement:**

1. Remove front wheel (see Section 3F-4)
2. Remove brake caliper retaining bolts (2 bolts per caliper)
3. Support caliper with wire to prevent damage to brake hose
4. Remove old brake pads from caliper carrier
5. Remove wear indicators from old pads if equipped
6. Install wear indicators on new pads in same location
7. Install new brake pads in caliper carrier
8. Reinstall caliper and tighten bolts to specification:
   - Brake caliper bolts: 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)
9. Pump brake pedal several times to establish brake pad contact
10. Install wheel and tighten to specification

**Rear Brake Pad Replacement:**

1. Remove rear wheel (see Section 3F-4)
2. Remove brake caliper retaining bolts
3. Support caliper with wire
4. Remove old brake pads
5. Transfer wear indicators to new pads if equipped
6. Install new brake pads in caliper carrier
7. Reinstall caliper and tighten to specification:
   - Brake caliper bolts: 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)
8. Pump brake pedal to establish contact
9. Install wheel

**Important Notes:**
- Always replace pads in axle sets (both front or both rear together)
- Do not mix old and new pads on same axle
- Pump brake pedal before road test to ensure proper pad contact
- Test brake function in safe area before full operation

**Friction Pad Specifications:**
- Material: Organic or semi-metallic approved material
- Wear indicator: Audible type on some applications
- Installation: Use manufacturer-approved pads or equivalent

## Parking Brake Inspection and Adjustment

### Parking Brake Efficiency Inspection

**Inspection Procedure:**

1. Apply parking brake while vehicle is parked on level surface
2. Check that parking brake lever engages fully without excessive effort
3. Count ratchet clicks as lever is pulled fully:
   - Normal engagement: Point 7 of ratchet mechanism
   - If engagement occurs before point 7, brake needs adjustment
   - If more than point 7 is needed, brake cable may be stretched

**Parking Brake Function Check:**

1. On level surface, engage parking brake fully
2. Attempt to move vehicle forward under light engine power
3. Vehicle should not move (holding power)
4. Release parking brake and verify brake lights extinguish

### Parking Brake Adjustment Procedure

**Lever Height Adjustment:**

1. Remove parking brake console or cover as required to access adjustment points
2. Locate brake cable adjuster at parking brake lever base
3. Loosen lock nut on adjuster bolt
4. Rotate adjuster bolt to modify brake engagement point:
   - Clockwise rotation: Increases cable tension (earlier engagement)
   - Counter-clockwise rotation: Decreases cable tension (later engagement)
5. Adjust until parking brake engages at point 7 of ratchet
6. Tighten lock nut to secure adjustment
7. Test engagement and holding power

**Cable Substitution (See section below for detailed cable replacement)**

**Specifications:**
- Normal engagement point: Point 7 of ratchet mechanism
- Brake fluid pressure (parking brake system): Mechanical (no hydraulic pressure)
- Holding capability: Vehicle must not move on 20% grade

## Brake Fluid Substitution and Circuit Purging

### Brake Fluid Specifications

**Approved Fluid Type:**
- DOT 4 brake fluid (Refer to Section 0B-17 for specific fluid recommendations)
- Fluid boiling point: Minimum 230°C (wet) / 311°C (dry)

**Important Warnings:**
- Use only approved brake fluid
- Do not mix different fluid types
- Brake fluid absorbs moisture; replace completely every 2 years regardless of use
- Never reuse old brake fluid
- Brake fluid is toxic; avoid skin contact and ingestion

### Brake Fluid Level Inspection

1. Park vehicle on level surface
2. Locate master cylinder reservoir in engine compartment
3. Clean area around reservoir cap to prevent contamination
4. Remove reservoir cap carefully (may have diaphragm or spring-loaded cap)
5. Visually inspect fluid level:
   - Should be at MIN or MAX marking on reservoir
   - Add fluid if level is below MIN
   - If fluid must be added frequently, inspect system for leaks

### Complete Brake Fluid Substitution Procedure

**System Purging (Bleeding) Steps:**

1. Locate all four brake wheel cylinders or calipers (front left, front right, rear left, rear right)
2. Identify bleeder screw locations on each caliper/cylinder
3. Prepare collection container for old brake fluid
4. Gather required equipment:
   - Bleeder wrench (typically 8 mm or 10 mm)
   - Clear vinyl tubing (fits snugly on bleeder screw)
   - Collection bottle or suitable container
   - New DOT 4 brake fluid (full bottle)

**Bleeding Sequence:**

The proper bleeding sequence prevents air pockets. Brake circuits vary by model; typical sequence:

1. Start at master cylinder reservoir location
2. Open first wheel cylinder bleeder screw by 1/2 to 1 turn
3. Install vinyl tubing on bleeder screw, submerge free end in clean brake fluid in collection container
4. Have assistant press brake pedal slowly to bottom
5. Close bleeder screw while pedal is depressed (while assistant maintains pressure)
6. Allow pedal to return slowly
7. Repeat steps 4-6 until fluid flowing from tube shows no air bubbles (typically 8-12 pump cycles)
8. Close bleeder screw firmly (do not over-tighten)
9. Move to next wheel cylinder following recommended sequence

**Circuit Purging Sequence (Typical):**
1. Master cylinder (if equipped with bleeder screw)
2. Brake circuit 1 (typically front left or rear left depending on circuit configuration)
3. Brake circuit 2 (cross-circuit wheel)
4. Brake circuit 3 (remaining wheels following dual-circuit pattern)

**System Level Maintenance:**

1. Monitor master cylinder reservoir level throughout bleeding process
2. Keep reservoir filled to proper level at all times
3. Never allow reservoir to empty completely (introduces air into system)
4. Add new DOT 4 fluid as level drops
5. When all wheels are bled and flowing clear fluid, stop and inspect:
   - All bleeder screws closed and tight
   - No fluid leaks at any connection
   - Brake pedal feels firm with no sponginess

**Final Steps:**

1. Pump brake pedal 10-15 times to establish full system pressure
2. Verify brake pedal is firm with no spongy feel
3. Test brakes in safe area with low-speed stops
4. If pedal remains spongy, repeat bleeding procedure
5. Properly dispose of old brake fluid (hazardous waste)

## Master Cylinder Inspection and Service

### Master Cylinder General Description

The master cylinder is the primary hydraulic pump for the brake system. It converts mechanical brake pedal pressure into hydraulic pressure distributed to front and rear brake circuits. A dual-circuit design provides safety if one circuit fails.

**Components:**
1. Primary piston (front brake circuit)
2. Secondary piston (rear brake circuit)
3. Internal return springs
4. Sealing rings (cup seals)
5. Piston rod connection from brake pedal
6. Reservoir with MIN/MAX level indicators
7. Compensator ports (maintain proper fluid level)

### Master Cylinder Inspection Procedure

**Visual Inspection:**

1. Park vehicle on level surface
2. Open hood and locate master cylinder (mounted on bulkhead above brake pedal)
3. Inspect exterior for:
   - Brake fluid leaks at any point
   - Fluid level in reservoir (should be at MAX when cold)
   - Cleanliness of reservoir
4. Check brake lines connected to cylinder for leaks or damage
5. Inspect mounting bolts for tightness

**Fluid Condition Check:**

1. Remove reservoir cap carefully
2. Visually inspect fluid:
   - Color should be clear to pale yellow
   - Dark or cloudy fluid indicates water absorption or contamination
   - Odor should be absent or minimally chemical
3. If fluid condition is questionable, perform complete fluid substitution (see section above)

### Master Cylinder Purging Procedure

If the master cylinder becomes air-bound (contains trapped air bubbles), the brake pedal will feel spongy and brakes may not develop full pressure.

**Purging Steps:**

1. Fill master cylinder reservoir with new DOT 4 brake fluid
2. Locate bleeder screw on master cylinder (if equipped; may be integrated)
3. If no master cylinder bleeder screw, proceed with full system bleeding as described in Brake Fluid Substitution section
4. If equipped with bleeder screw:
   - Attach clear vinyl tubing to bleeder screw
   - Submerge tube end in clean brake fluid
   - Slowly press brake pedal fully
   - Open bleeder screw for 1 second while pedal is down
   - Close bleeder screw and allow pedal to return
   - Repeat 5-6 times until no air bubbles appear in fluid
5. Tighten bleeder screw securely when complete

**Verification:**

1. Pump brake pedal 10-15 times
2. Verify pedal feels firm (not spongy)
3. Perform full brake circuit purging as described in Brake Fluid Substitution section if sponginess persists

### Master Cylinder Seal Replacement

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Remove master cylinder from vehicle (requires brake line disconnection)
2. Place on clean work bench
3. Remove primary piston snap ring or retaining screw
4. Remove primary piston assembly
5. Remove secondary piston assembly
6. Inspect all seals and springs
7. Clean all internal surfaces with isopropyl alcohol and lint-free cloth

**Installation Procedure:**

1. Acquire replacement seal kit (includes all seals and springs)
2. Install new seals on pistons following kit instructions
3. Lubricate seals lightly with clean brake fluid
4. Install secondary piston assembly into cylinder bore
5. Install primary piston assembly on top
6. Secure with retaining snap ring or screw
7. Reinstall master cylinder on vehicle
8. Connect brake lines with proper torque
9. Fill reservoir and perform system purging (see Brake Fluid Substitution section)

## Vacuum Circuit Checking and Procedures

### Vacuum Circuit Purpose

The vacuum circuit supplies vacuum-assisted power to the brake booster. This system reduces brake pedal effort required to stop the vehicle. A loss of vacuum results in very hard brake pedal and increased stopping distance.

### Vacuum System Components

1. Engine intake manifold (vacuum source)
2. Vacuum hoses and connections
3. Vacuum check valve (one-way)
4. Brake booster assembly
5. Air filter (at booster input, if equipped)

### Vacuum Level Checking Procedure

**Equipped Vacuum Gauge Check:**

1. Start engine and allow to reach normal operating temperature
2. Locate vacuum gauge port on booster or engine (if equipped)
3. Connect vacuum gauge to port
4. Note vacuum reading:
   - Normal vacuum at idle: 850 mbar (650 mm Hg)
   - Acceptable range: 800-900 mbar
   - Below 800 mbar indicates system issue

**If Vacuum Reading is Low:**

1. Check all vacuum hoses for:
   - Cracks or splits
   - Loose connections at either end
   - Proper routing without sharp bends
2. Verify vacuum check valve operation:
   - Allows vacuum from engine to booster
   - Prevents vacuum loss if engine stops
   - If check valve is stuck closed, no vacuum reaches booster
3. Inspect booster for internal leaks:
   - With engine off, press brake pedal repeatedly
   - Pedal should get progressively firmer (harder) with each press
   - If pedal effort does not increase, booster is leaking internally

### Vacuum Hose Replacement Procedure

1. Note routing of vacuum hose before removal (take photo if necessary)
2. Loosen hose clamp at one end and slide hose off
3. Loosen hose clamp at other end and remove old hose
4. Inspect connections for cracks or damage
5. Install new vacuum hose rated for brake booster service
6. Route hose following original path without sharp bends
7. Secure hose with clamps at both ends
8. Verify no kinks or pinched areas exist
9. Start engine and check for vacuum hose being sucked inward (normal indication of system function)

**Vacuum Hose Specifications:**
- Material: Reinforced rubber rated for brake system service
- Diameter: 6 mm to 8 mm (check original hose diameter)
- Length: Must be sufficient to reach from source to booster without tension

## Brake Pipes and Hoses Substitution

### General Description

The brake system uses both rigid steel pipes (lines) and flexible hoses. Steel pipes form the main brake circuit runs. Flexible hoses connect to wheel cylinders and calipers (which move with suspension).

**Components:**
- Steel brake lines (runs along frame)
- Brake hoses (connect wheel cylinders/calipers to fixed lines)
- Hose clips and supports
- Banjo fittings (for hose connection points)
- Flare fittings (for line connections)

### Brake Hose Inspection

**Visual Inspection:**

1. Inspect all accessible brake hoses for:
   - Cracks or splits in outer cover
   - Soft spots or swelling (internal failure)
   - Separation of hose plies
   - Color fading or hardening (age deterioration)
2. Check hose routing:
   - Hoses should not touch sharp edges
   - Hoses should not be twisted or kinked
   - Adequate clearance from exhaust components
3. Check all hose clamp connections for tightness
4. Inspect banjo fitting connections for leaks

**Age-Based Replacement:**
- Replace all brake hoses every 5 years regardless of appearance
- Replace immediately if any damage is observed
- Replace if hose has been subjected to high heat

### Brake Hose Replacement Procedure

**Removal:**

1. Place drain pan under hose connection point
2. Close brake fluid supply if available, or perform full reservoir purge afterward
3. Disconnect hose from wheel cylinder or caliper:
   - Use two wrenches (one to hold fitting, one to turn hose)
   - Support brake cylinder/caliper to prevent strain
   - Brake fluid will drip; have absorbent material ready
4. Disconnect hose from main brake line:
   - Note fitting type (banjo or flare)
   - Use two wrenches to disconnect
5. Remove hose clamps and withdraw hose
6. Inspect hose routing path for obstruction

**Installation:**

1. Obtain replacement hose of correct length and specification
2. Verify hose has correct thread sizes and fitting types
3. Install clamps on new hose at original mounting locations
4. Connect hose to main brake line first:
   - Use two wrenches to tighten
   - Do not over-tighten (risk of cracking fitting)
   - Torque specification: 1.8 + 2.2 Kg-m (18 + 22 Nm)
5. Connect hose to wheel cylinder or caliper:
   - Align fitting carefully
   - Use two wrenches to tighten
   - Torque specification: 2.5 + 3.0 Kg-m (25 + 30 Nm)
6. Secure hose clamps to prevent movement
7. Inspect routing for any sharp bends or contact points
8. Fill master cylinder reservoir with DOT 4 brake fluid
9. Perform system purging (bleeding) as described in Brake Fluid Substitution section

### Brake Line (Steel Pipe) Replacement Procedure

**Removal:**

1. Locate damaged brake line section
2. Identify connection points at each end
3. Use two wrenches to disconnect flare fittings:
   - One wrench holds the fitting body
   - One wrench turns the nut
4. Remove line clips and supports along routing path
5. Withdraw damaged line carefully

**Installation:**

1. Obtain replacement brake line of identical specification:
   - Check diameter (typically 4.75 mm or 6.35 mm)
   - Verify length measurement from fittings
   - Confirm flare fitting thread sizes
2. Install line following original routing path:
   - Use existing clips and supports
   - Ensure no sharp bends
   - Verify adequate clearance from hot components
   - Avoid crossing electrical harnesses
3. Connect line at one end using two wrenches
4. Connect line at other end
5. Torque specifications for flare fittings:
   - Line to master cylinder: 1.8 + 2.2 Kg-m (18 + 22 Nm)
   - Line to wheel cylinder: 1.8 + 2.2 Kg-m (18 + 22 Nm)
   - Line to proportioning valve: 1.8 + 2.2 Kg-m (18 + 22 Nm)
6. Secure line clips to prevent vibration
7. Fill master cylinder reservoir
8. Perform system purging as described in Brake Fluid Substitution section

## Parking Brake Lever and Cable Substitution

### General Description

The parking brake uses a mechanical cable system to apply brake pressure to the rear wheels. The system includes a lever (controlled by driver), cable runs, and connection to rear brake mechanical linkages.

**Components:**
1. Parking brake lever (console or floor-mounted)
2. Primary cable (lever to cable junction)
3. Secondary cables (left and right to rear wheels)
4. Cable guides and supports
5. Rear brake mechanical linkage
6. Ratchet and hold mechanism

### Parking Brake Lever Removal and Installation

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Remove parking brake console or floor cover as required to access lever
2. Disconnect electrical connector if lever-mounted warning light switch is present
3. Locate cable attachment point at lever base
4. Disconnect primary cable from lever (may require removing retaining pin or clip)
5. Remove lever mounting bolts (typically 2-4 bolts)
6. Withdraw lever assembly from vehicle

**Installation Procedure:**

1. Position new lever assembly in mounting location
2. Install mounting bolts:
   - Torque: 1.5 + 2.0 Kg-m (15 + 20 Nm)
3. Reconnect primary cable to lever attachment point
4. Reconnect electrical connector if present
5. Reinstall console or cover
6. Test lever operation and ratchet engagement

### Parking Brake Cable Substitution

**Cable Routing Identification:**

Before removal, identify complete cable routing:
- Primary cable path from lever to cable junction
- Secondary cable paths to left and right rear wheels
- All support clips and routing guides
- Connection points at rear brake mechanism

**Removal Procedure:**

1. Locate cable access points at rear wheels
2. Disconnect secondary cable from rear brake mechanism:
   - May require removal of brake drum or caliper
   - Typically connected with retaining pin or clevis pin
3. Remove cable routing clips along body
4. Pull cable forward through firewall
5. At lever end, disconnect primary cable from lever (if not already done in lever removal)
6. Withdraw cable completely from vehicle

**Installation Procedure:**

1. Obtain replacement cable of identical specification
2. Route new primary cable from lever area toward rear:
   - Thread through firewall
   - Use original routing guides and clips
   - Ensure no sharp bends or pinches
3. Secure cable with clips along routing path
4. Connect secondary cables at rear wheels:
   - Left rear connection
   - Right rear connection
   - Use retaining or clevis pins to secure connections
5. Connect primary cable to lever base
6. Adjust cable tension using adjustment procedure (see Parking Brake Adjustment section)
7. Test parking brake engagement and holding capability

**Cable Specifications:**
- Material: Steel wire core with protective sheath
- Flexibility: Must allow full travel without kinking
- Attachment: Clevis pins or retaining clips

## Brake Caliper Assembly and Repair

### Brake Caliper General Description

Brake calipers house the friction pads and apply braking force to the disc. Calipers operate with hydraulic pressure from the master cylinder. Proper caliper operation is essential for effective braking.

**Caliper Types:**
- Fixed calipers: Mounted solidly to axle housing
- Floating calipers: Mounted on guides, able to move slightly
- Single-piston: One piston applies force
- Multi-piston: Two or more pistons (typically front calipers)

**Components:**
1. Caliper housing (aluminum or iron)
2. Pistons (typically 1-2 per caliper)
3. Sealing rings (prevent fluid leakage)
4. Dust boots (prevent contamination)
5. Brake pad carrier
6. Mounting guides or pins
7. Bleed screw (for system purging)
8. Wear indicators (audible or visual)

### Brake Caliper Removal Procedure

**Removal Steps:**

1. Place vehicle on lift or appropriate support
2. Remove wheel from affected side (see Section 3F-4)
3. Remove brake pads (see Brake Pad Replacement section)
4. Disconnect brake hose from caliper:
   - Place drain pan to catch brake fluid
   - Use two wrenches to disconnect banjo fitting
   - Torque: 2.5 + 3.0 Kg-m (25 + 30 Nm)
   - Immediately plug hose end to prevent fluid loss
5. Support brake hose with wire to prevent damage
6. Remove caliper mounting bolts:
   - Typically 2 bolts holding caliper to mounting bracket
   - Torque specification: 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)
7. Withdraw caliper assembly from vehicle

### Brake Caliper Installation Procedure

1. Position caliper on mounting bracket
2. Install mounting bolts and tighten to specification:
   - Torque: 3.1 + 3.5 Kg-m (31 + 35 Nm)
3. Remove plug from brake hose
4. Connect brake hose to caliper:
   - Use two wrenches to tighten banjo fitting
   - Torque: 2.5 + 3.0 Kg-m (25 + 30 Nm)
5. Install brake pads (see Brake Pad Replacement section)
6. Pump brake pedal multiple times to establish brake pressure
7. Verify no leaks at brake hose connection
8. Install wheel and tighten to specification:
   - Torque: 11 + 17 Kg-m (110 + 170 Nm)
9. Perform system purging if brake pedal is spongy

### Brake Caliper Piston Removal and Inspection

**Piston Removal (with caliper removed from vehicle):**

1. Place caliper on clean work surface
2. Using brake fluid in a small container, press piston into caliper:
   - Introduce compressed air at brake hose connection (low pressure, 3-4 bar)
   - Piston will be forced out of caliper bore
   - Have absorbent material ready to catch fluid and piston
3. Withdraw piston carefully
4. Repeat for additional pistons if caliper has multiple pistons
5. Remove and inspect sealing rings

**Piston Inspection:**

1. Visually inspect piston surface for:
   - Corrosion or pitting
   - Scoring or scratches
   - Discoloration from heat
2. Check piston bore (inside caliper) for:
   - Scoring or damage
   - Corrosion
   - Contamination
3. Determine if piston is serviceable:
   - Minor surface corrosion may be cleaned with fine crocus cloth
   - Deep pitting or scoring requires complete caliper replacement
   - If bore is damaged, caliper body must be replaced

### Brake Caliper Seal Replacement

**Seal Removal and Cleaning:**

1. Remove sealing rings from piston grooves:
   - Use soft plastic tools to avoid scratching
   - Note orientation of seal rings
2. Inspect seal grooves for damage
3. Clean all internal passages with isopropyl alcohol
4. Allow to air dry completely

**Seal Installation:**

1. Obtain replacement sealing rings (included in brake caliper rebuild kit)
2. Lightly lubricate new seals with fresh brake fluid
3. Install new sealing rings in grooves:
   - Ensure rings sit fully in grooves
   - Rings should not be twisted or pinched
4. Install dust boots on pistons
5. Install piston back into caliper bore:
   - Guide piston straight into bore
   - Do not force or allow piston to cock sideways
   - Piston should slide smoothly with finger pressure

### Brake Caliper Assembly Torque Specifications

| Component | Kg-m | Nm |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Caliper mounting bolts | 3.1 + 3.5 | 31 + 35 |
| Brake hose banjo fitting | 2.5 + 3.0 | 25 + 30 |
| Bleed screw | 0.5 + 0.7 | 5 + 7 |
| Piston retaining bolt (if applicable) | 1.0 + 1.5 | 10 + 15 |

## LSPV (Load Sensing Pressure Valve) Assembly

### LSPV General Description

The Load Sensing Pressure Valve (LSPV) is a proportioning valve that adjusts brake pressure distribution between front and rear brake circuits based on vehicle load. This system ensures optimal braking balance whether the vehicle is lightly loaded or fully loaded.

**Function:**
- Senses vehicle load through suspension geometry or electrical sensors
- Modulates rear brake pressure proportionally
- Prevents rear wheel lock-up during heavy braking
- Improves brake performance and safety

**Components:**
1. Valve body (aluminum or iron)
2. Piston assembly
3. Spring stack (load-sensing element)
4. Internal passages for brake fluid distribution
5. Input ports (from master cylinder)
6. Output ports (to rear brake circuit)
7. Adjustment screw (if manually adjustable)

### LSPV Operation Principle

**Light Vehicle Load:**
- Suspension is higher (less compressed)
- Valve proportions more brake pressure to front wheels
- Rear wheels have reduced pressure (prevents lock-up)

**Heavy Vehicle Load:**
- Suspension is lower (more compressed)
- Valve increases brake pressure to rear wheels proportionally
- Front and rear braking force becomes more equal

**Result:**
- Improved stopping efficiency at all load states
- Reduced rear wheel lock-up tendency
- Better vehicle control during emergency braking

### LSPV Inspection and Testing

**Visual Inspection:**

1. Locate LSPV on vehicle (typically mounted on or near rear axle housing)
2. Check for:
   - Brake fluid leaks at any fitting
   - Corrosion or damage to valve body
   - Loose mounting bolts
3. Verify all brake lines are connected securely

**Functional Testing:**

Proper LSPV testing requires brake pressure measurement equipment:

1. Install pressure gauges in front and rear brake circuits
2. With vehicle unloaded (no passengers or cargo):
   - Apply brake pressure gradually
   - Observe front and rear pressure readings
   - Rear pressure should be proportionally lower than front
3. With vehicle fully loaded:
   - Repeat pressure test
   - Rear pressure should increase relative to front pressure
4. If readings are abnormal, LSPV may require adjustment or replacement

### LSPV Adjustment Procedure

**Manual Adjustment (if equipped with adjustment screw):**

1. Locate adjustment screw on LSPV body
2. Remove protective cap if present
3. Note current position of adjustment screw
4. Turn adjustment screw:
   - Clockwise: Increases rear brake pressure
   - Counter-clockwise: Decreases rear brake pressure
5. Make small adjustments (1/4 turn maximum per test)
6. Retest brake pressure balance after each adjustment
7. Once balanced, secure adjustment screw and install protective cap

**If Adjustment is Not Possible:**

The LSPV may be a sealed, non-adjustable design. In this case:
- Verify operation with pressure testing
- If function is abnormal, replace complete LSPV assembly

### LSPV Replacement Procedure

**Removal:**

1. Place drain pan under LSPV connections
2. Disconnect all brake lines from LSPV:
   - Identify which lines are inputs and outputs
   - Use two wrenches to disconnect each fitting
   - Note fluid volume (will leak when lines are removed)
3. Disconnect LSPV from mounting bracket:
   - Remove mounting bolts (typically 2-4 bolts)
4. Withdraw LSPV assembly from vehicle

**Installation:**

1. Position new LSPV in mounting location
2. Install mounting bolts:
   - Torque specification: 2.5 + 3.0 Kg-m (25 + 30 Nm)
3. Connect all brake lines to correct ports:
   - Verify correct input and output line connections (refer to diagram)
   - Use two wrenches to tighten each fitting
   - Torque specification: 1.8 + 2.2 Kg-m (18 + 22 Nm)
4. Fill master cylinder reservoir with DOT 4 brake fluid
5. Perform complete system purging (bleeding) as described in Brake Fluid Substitution section
6. Perform brake pressure testing to verify proper operation
7. Test vehicle braking on safe surface before full operation

**LSPV Torque Specifications:**

| Component | Kg-m | Nm |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Mounting bolts | 2.5 + 3.0 | 25 + 30 |
| Brake line fittings | 1.8 + 2.2 | 18 + 22 |

## Brake Booster Service and Testing

### Brake Booster General Description

The brake booster uses engine vacuum to provide power assistance, reducing brake pedal effort. A loss of booster function results in very hard brake pedal and significantly increased stopping distance.

**Components:**
1. Vacuum inlet (from engine)
2. Check valve (one-way vacuum flow)
3. Booster chamber (vacuum-sealed)
4. Piston assembly
5. Piston rod (connected to master cylinder)
6. Input shaft (connected to brake pedal)
7. Seals and gaskets
8. Atmospheric inlet valve

### Brake Booster Operation Check

**With Engine Running:**

1. Start engine and allow to reach idle
2. Press brake pedal slowly with moderate pressure
3. Observe pedal travel distance
4. Repeat depressing pedal with engine running 3-4 times
5. Stop engine (do not press brake pedal yet)
6. With engine off, press brake pedal:
   - Pedal should move normally first press
   - Pedal should become progressively harder with each additional press
   - This indicates booster reserve vacuum is depleting

**Expected Results:**
- Engine running: Light pedal effort with moderate travel
- Engine off, first press: Normal effort
- Engine off, subsequent presses: Progressively harder effort

**Abnormal Results:**
- Pedal very hard with engine running = Booster not functioning
- Pedal does not get harder with engine off = Booster check valve leaking
- Pedal has no effect = Loss of vacuum connection

### Brake Booster Vacuum Check

**Vacuum Hose Inspection:**

1. Locate vacuum hose from engine to booster
2. Check for:
   - Cracks or splits in hose
   - Soft spots or deterioration
   - Disconnected or loose connections
3. If hose is defective, replace following procedure in Vacuum Circuit Checking section

**Check Valve Testing:**

1. Locate check valve (typically on vacuum hose near booster)
2. Verify vacuum flows only one direction (from engine to booster)
3. If check valve is stuck or leaking, replace with new valve
4. Torque valve connections: 1.5 + 2.0 Kg-m (15 + 20 Nm)

### Brake Booster Replacement Procedure

**Removal:**

1. Locate brake booster (mounted on bulkhead behind master cylinder)
2. Disconnect vacuum hose from booster inlet
3. From inside vehicle (under dashboard), disconnect booster input rod from brake pedal:
   - May require removal of clevis pin or retaining bolt
4. Disconnect brake lines from master cylinder (which is mounted on booster):
   - Use two wrenches for each fitting
   - Place drain pan to catch fluid
5. Remove booster mounting bolts (typically 4 bolts):
   - Bolts are accessed from inside vehicle
   - Remove booster assembly as unit with master cylinder
6. Once removed from vehicle, separate booster from master cylinder:
   - Remove bolts connecting master cylinder to booster
   - Booster and master cylinder can be serviced separately

**Installation:**

1. Position booster in mounting location
2. Connect master cylinder to booster:
   - Install all mounting bolts
   - Torque: 2.0 + 2.5 Kg-m (20 + 25 Nm)
3. Install booster assembly into vehicle:
   - Position booster correctly on bulkhead
   - Install mounting bolts from inside
   - Torque: 2.0 + 2.5 Kg-m (20 + 25 Nm)
4. Connect booster input rod to brake pedal:
   - Align clevis pin holes
   - Secure with clevis pin and retaining clip
5. Connect vacuum hose to booster inlet
6. Connect brake lines to master cylinder:
   - Use two wrenches
   - Torque: 1.8 + 2.2 Kg-m (18 + 22 Nm)
7. Fill master cylinder reservoir with DOT 4 brake fluid
8. Perform complete system purging as described in Brake Fluid Substitution section
9. Test brake booster operation and verify proper pedal feel

**Booster Mounting Torque Specifications:**

| Component | Kg-m | Nm |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Booster mounting bolts | 2.0 + 2.5 | 20 + 25 |
| Master cylinder to booster | 2.0 + 2.5 | 20 + 25 |
| Brake line fittings | 1.8 + 2.2 | 18 + 22 |

## System Testing and Verification

### Road Test Procedure

After completing any brake service work, perform the following road test:

**Low-Speed Brake Test:**

1. In empty parking lot, accelerate to 15-20 km/h
2. Apply brakes smoothly with moderate pressure
3. Observe:
   - Vehicle stops in expected distance
   - Vehicle does not pull to either side
   - Brake pedal feels firm (not spongy)
   - No unusual noises

**Progressive Braking Test:**

1. Accelerate to 30 km/h
2. Apply brakes with gradually increasing pressure
3. Observe pedal travel and effort required
4. Vehicle should stop smoothly with no pulling

**Emergency Braking Test:**

1. Accelerate to 40 km/h
2. Apply full brake pressure quickly
3. Observe:
   - Maximum deceleration is achieved
   - Vehicle does not lock wheels (ABS will pulse if equipped)
   - No pulling to either side

**Parking Brake Test:**

1. Park on slight slope
2. Engage parking brake fully
3. Attempt to move vehicle under light power
4. Vehicle should remain stationary

### Final Verification Checklist

- All brake pads installed and properly seated
- All brake discs within specification
- All brake lines and hoses connected without leaks
- Brake fluid at correct level and clear in color
- Brake pedal feels firm with no sponginess
- Parking brake engages at point 7 of ratchet
- No warning lights active on dashboard
- Vacuum booster provides power assistance
- No unusual brake noises during test drive
- Vehicle stops evenly without pulling to either side
- Parking brake holds vehicle on slope

## Important Safety Cautions

### General Warnings

- The brake system is safety-critical. Improper service can result in brake failure and serious accident.
- Never work under a vehicle supported only by a jack. Always use jack stands.
- Always support the vehicle securely on trestles when working underneath.
- Ensure adequate lighting in work area.
- Wear safety glasses and gloves when handling brake fluid.

### Brake Fluid Hazards

- Brake fluid is toxic; avoid skin contact and ingestion.
- Brake fluid is hygroscopic (absorbs moisture); store in sealed containers.
- Brake fluid damages paint; wipe spills immediately.
- Never reuse brake fluid that has been bled from the system.
- Dispose of old brake fluid as hazardous waste.

### Brake System Service Precautions

- Never press brake pedal with brake lines disconnected.
- Always reconnect all brake lines before operating vehicle.
- Never allow brake hose to hang from brake cylinder connections.
- Support brake hoses with wire when disconnected.
- Do not allow brake system to remain without fluid.
- Keep brake fluid reservoir filled during all bleeding procedures.
- Never leave brake system partially disassembled.
- Always perform complete system testing before normal vehicle operation.

### Component Handling

- Handle brake discs carefully; wear gloves to prevent skin oils from contaminating surfaces.
- Do not drop or strike brake components.
- Never use heat to loosen blocked fasteners on brake components.
- Inspect brake hoses for damage before installation.
- Verify all seals are properly seated before assembly.

### Electrical Safety

- Disconnect battery negative terminal before working with electrical brake components (booster, LSPV sensors).
- Inspect electrical connectors for corrosion before reconnection.
- Never apply excessive pressure to electrical connections.
